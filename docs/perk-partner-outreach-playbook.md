# NILPro · Perk Partner Outreach Playbook

**Audience:** You (founder) and anyone helping with partner recruitment
**Purpose:** Recruit local businesses to join NILPro's perk network
**Goal per market:** 8–15 active perk partners before opening that market for athlete signups

---

## The pitch in one sentence

> NILPro athletes get exclusive discounts at local businesses in their hometown — we're inviting Tony's Pizza to be one of them, free of charge, in exchange for foot traffic and visibility with verified local high school and college athletes and their families.

---

## Why local businesses say yes (the real reasons)

Don't lead with these in the email, but understand them so you can address them in conversation:

1. **Foot traffic at zero cost.** They give a discount; they don't pay anything to participate.
2. **Marketing to a specific, valuable demographic.** Athletes + their parents + their teammates + their friends = exactly the local family customers small businesses want.
3. **Community goodwill.** "Supporting local athletes" is something every small business owner is already inclined toward.
4. **Low commitment.** They can pause or leave any time.
5. **Optional upside.** Some will eventually want to do real sponsorships — being a perk partner first is the easy entry point.

---

## What you're asking for

Be specific. Don't leave the offer fuzzy.

**The standard offer:**
- 10–20% off for verified NILPro athletes (their choice)
- OR a specific freebie (e.g., "free first cut," "free drink with any meal")
- Honored when athlete shows their NILPro app at checkout
- No exclusivity required — they can offer the same discount elsewhere
- Free to participate
- Can pause or withdraw any time

**What partners commit to:**
- Honoring the offer when athletes present it
- Setting a monthly cap (e.g., "I can handle 30 athletes/month") that NILPro respects
- Letting NILPro list them in the partner directory

**What partners do NOT commit to:**
- Paying NILPro anything
- Being a sponsor of any specific athlete
- Long-term contract
- Exclusivity

This low-commitment posture is the entire reason this works. Don't let scope creep into your pitches.

## Cold email pitch template (primary)

Use this for cold outreach to local business owner emails. Adjust the {bracketed} fields.

---

**Subject:** Free way to drive foot traffic from {city} athletes

Hi {first_name},

I'm reaching out because {business} is the kind of local spot we'd love to feature in our local deals network for {city}-area student athletes.

Quick context: NILPro is a platform that helps high school and small-college athletes earn from sponsorship deals. We've also built a side network where local businesses offer small discounts (think 10–20% off) to our verified athletes — they're real members, not random people, and the discounts are visible only inside our app.

What you'd get:
- Foot traffic from athletes, their families, and their friends
- Visibility in our app to every NILPro athlete in the {city} area
- Zero cost to participate, no contract, leave anytime

What we'd ask for:
- A discount of your choice (most partners do 10–20% off, some do a freebie like a free first item)
- Honored when an athlete shows the app at checkout
- A monthly cap you set so you're never overwhelmed

We're building the network in {city} now. If you're interested, just reply and I'll send a one-page summary with everything spelled out — takes about 5 minutes to get set up.

If not a fit, no worries — appreciate you reading.

Best,
{your_name}
NILPro

---

### Why this email works

- **Subject is concrete.** "Free way to drive foot traffic" is specific value, not "exciting partnership opportunity."
- **Local reference up top.** "{city}-area student athletes" — establishes you know their geography.
- **States what NILPro is in one sentence.** Don't overexplain. Most owners don't need a deep dive.
- **Specifies what they get and what they give in clean lists.** Removes ambiguity.
- **Acknowledges low commitment.** "No contract, leave anytime" preempts the #1 objection.
- **Has a real, small ask.** Reply to get a one-pager. Not "let's hop on a 30-minute call."
- **Polite exit.** Respects their time if not interested.

Length: ~140 words. Don't go longer.

## Cold email pitch template (alternative — for businesses already known to be athlete-friendly)

For businesses near schools, sporting goods stores, athletic apparel, etc.

---

**Subject:** {school_name} athletes — local deal partner?

Hi {first_name},

Quick one — I work with NILPro, a platform helping {school_name} and other local athletes earn from sponsorships. We're putting together a network of local businesses that offer small discounts to our verified athletes.

We thought of {business} because you're close to campus and athletes already come through. The deal: you offer a small discount (whatever you're comfortable with — usually 10–20% off), athletes see it in their app, you get foot traffic from a tight-knit community.

It's free, no contract, and you set a monthly cap so it doesn't get out of hand.

Worth a quick reply if you'd like to hear more?

Thanks,
{your_name}

---

Length: ~100 words. Even shorter for owners who'll skim.

## In-person pitch (if you walk in)

For walking into local businesses cold. Adapt to conversation, but here's the structure:

> "Hey, I'm {your_name}, I run NILPro — we help local high school and small-college athletes get paid through sponsorship deals.
>
> We're building a network of local businesses in {city} that offer small discounts to our verified athletes — like 10 or 20 percent off. The athlete shows the app, gets the discount, and you get foot traffic from local families. It's free, no contract, you set a monthly cap.
>
> Would something like that be a fit for you, or worth talking about?"

If they ask follow-ups:
- **"How do I know they're real athletes?"** "Every NILPro athlete is verified — we confirm their school, sport, and identity before they're on the platform. The app shows their verified badge when they redeem."
- **"How does redemption work?"** "Right now, athletes show their NILPro app at checkout — it has their name, photo, verified badge, and a redemption code. You honor the discount, and they self-report it in the app so we can send you a monthly summary."
- **"What if I get overwhelmed with athletes?"** "You set a monthly cap when you sign up — could be 20, 50, 100, whatever you want. We don't send athletes past that cap."
- **"What's the catch?"** "Honestly, none. Long-term we hope some partners want to do real sponsorship deals with specific athletes — but that's optional, separate, and only if it's a fit. The perk network is just the perk network."

## Target list — how to build it

For each market you want to launch:

1. **Pull a list of local businesses near the athlete's schools.** Use Google Maps, Yelp, or just walk around the area near the school.
2. **Filter to athlete-relevant categories:**
   - Casual food (pizza, burgers, sandwiches, tacos)
   - Coffee shops, smoothies, ice cream
   - Sporting goods stores (local, not Dick's)
   - Athletic apparel (local, not Nike)
   - Barbershops, hair salons
   - Gyms, training facilities, batting cages
   - Sports bars / family restaurants
   - PT / sports massage / chiropractors
3. **Find owner email** (not info@). Look at:
   - The business's "About" or "Contact" page
   - The business's Facebook page (often lists the owner directly)
   - The local Chamber of Commerce listing
   - LinkedIn for "Owner" or "President" of the business
4. **Verify the email** with a tool like ZeroBounce before sending — local businesses have a high bounce rate.
5. **Send in batches of 10–20**, not 100+ at once, so you can actually follow up on replies.

## Onboarding flow when a partner says yes

When a business replies "I'm interested" or "send me details":

### Step 1: Send the one-pager

A simple PDF or web page summarizing:
- What they offer (you and they decide)
- How redemption works
- Monthly cap setting
- How to pause or leave
- Their contact for questions

### Step 2: Get the offer specifics

A short form (or just an email reply):
- Business name (legal name + display name if different)
- Contact name + email + phone
- Address
- Discount offer (specific terms)
- Fine print (e.g., "dine-in only," "cannot combine with other offers")
- Monthly cap (default suggestion: 50)

### Step 3: Add to admin

You (or whoever runs partner ops) creates the partner record in your admin panel. Mark as `active`.

### Step 4: Welcome email

Send a simple welcome email confirming they're live, with:
- Their listing as it appears to athletes (preview)
- Their redemption confirmation page link (magic link, for v2 redemption tracking)
- Your direct contact for any questions

### Step 5: Add to monthly summary list

They'll get the automated monthly summary email going forward.

## Operating cadence

For each market, target this rhythm:

- **Weeks 1–4 (pre-launch):** 50 cold emails/week to local businesses. Follow up at 5 days. Goal: 8–15 signed partners by end of week 4.
- **Week 5+ (post-launch):** Continue recruiting at lower volume. Target: 1 new partner per week per market to keep the network growing.

When a partner pauses or churns, replace within 2 weeks. Markets with fewer than 5 active partners should pause new athlete signups (or use national fallback perks) to avoid empty week-1 drips.

---

## Categories of partner status

Track each partner in one of these states:

- **Active** — currently honoring offers
- **Paused** — temporarily not receiving athletes (their request)
- **Capped this month** — hit monthly redemption limit; will reactivate next month
- **Churned** — left the program

Active is the only state from which athletes can be matched.

---

## What to do if a partner has a bad experience

Possible failure modes:

1. **Athlete shows up with the app, partner doesn't recognize the discount.** Most likely a training issue if the partner has multiple staff. Send a partner-facing FAQ they can post at the register. Follow up if it happens twice.
2. **Athlete redeems too aggressively (e.g., 10 visits in a week).** Out-of-policy in spirit. Reach out to athlete, remind them of the spirit of the program. Cap individual redemptions at 1/week per perk if this becomes common.
3. **Partner reports athletes are bringing non-NILPro friends and asking for the discount on their food too.** Clarify in your terms: discount applies only to the verified athlete's own item.
4. **Partner gets overwhelmed and complains.** Lower their monthly cap or pause them. Apologize. Offer to slow it down.

The goal is for partners to feel respected. They're doing you a favor; treat the relationship that way.

## Tracking

In a spreadsheet or your admin panel, track per-business:

- Business name
- Contact name, email, phone
- Source (how you found them)
- First email sent date
- Reply received date
- Status (cold / replied / signed / declined / no_response)
- Notes (any context from the conversation)

Goal at end of week 4 in a market: 100 cold emails sent → 20 replies → 10 signed partners. That's a realistic conversion rate for low-commitment offers to local businesses.

---

## When to hand this off

You should personally do the first 30–50 partner recruitments yourself across your first 1–2 markets. You'll learn:

- Which categories convert best (food vs. apparel vs. services)
- Which pitch lines land
- What objections come up most
- What the typical discount offer looks like
- Which markets are easier or harder

After that, this is the right job to hand off to a contractor or part-time hire — ideally someone with local connections in target markets, like a college student or recent grad.
