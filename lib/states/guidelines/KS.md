# Kansas — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** KSHSAA Rule 21 — Amateur Status and Name, Image, and Likeness — [link](https://www.kshsaa.org/Public/PDF/AmateurStatusNov2021.pdf)

## Overview

Kansas permits high school NIL under KSHSAA Rule 21, originally adopted in November 2021 and unanimously affirmed by KSHSAA directors in 2023. The rule's strictest line is that NIL **cannot** be used as a "recruiting inducement" — no transfer-driven payments, no enrollment-driven payments. It also blocks NIL activity during school hours and any reference to your high school athletic awards.

## Who can participate

Any KSHSAA student-athlete may participate. The compensation must flow to you as an individual, on the basis of your name, likeness, or image apart from school activities — not through your team, your school, or your coach.

## Before you sign a deal

- **Read the contract with a parent.** A minor's NIL contract requires parent/guardian co-signature in Kansas.
- **Verify the deal does not reference your school.** No school name, mascot, logo, uniform, or team reference. Nothing in the deal can imply a school endorsement.
- **Strip references to your high-school athletic awards.** KSHSAA prohibits NIL activity that references awards you won as a high school athlete.
- **Confirm the deal is not from a school employee, coach, or booster.** Anything with that scent is treated as a recruiting inducement.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Notify your athletic director in writing.** KSHSAA does not publish a fixed disclosure window in Rule 21, but written notice before the deal goes live is the safe practice and gives you a paper trail if KSHSAA later asks.
2. **Get parent/guardian co-signature.** Required for minors.
3. **Do nothing during school hours.** No content shoots, posts, or appearances during the school day.
4. **Avoid school and KSHSAA marks.** No school logos, mascots, uniforms, or KSHSAA logos in promotional work.
5. **Avoid school facilities.** No filming on school grounds.
6. **No reference to high-school athletic awards.** Do not list state titles, all-state honors, or KSHSAA awards in branded content.
7. **No professional contracts.** Signing a pro contract while in high school ends your KSHSAA eligibility.
8. **Keep records.** Contract, payments, posts, and AD notification.

## Banned deal categories

KSHSAA prohibits NIL activity tied to:

- **Tobacco.**
- **Alcohol.**
- **Gambling.**
- **Banned athletic substances.**
- **Illegal substances or activities.**

Also prohibited:

- **Recruiting inducements.** Any deal designed to bring you to or hold you at a school.
- **Use of school marks.** School name, logo, mascot, uniform, or KSHSAA marks.
- **Reference to high-school athletic awards.**
- **Activity during school hours.**
- **Professional contracts.**

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation of Rule 21 results in loss of KSHSAA eligibility. Recruiting-inducement violations are taken especially seriously and can affect your school as well as you. Restoration of eligibility typically requires terminating the deal, returning compensation, and a KSHSAA review.
