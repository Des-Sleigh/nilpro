# Arkansas — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions (limited window)
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** Arkansas Act 589 of 2023 + Arkansas Activities Association Handbook (Amateurism) — [link](https://www.arkansasactivities.org/Forms/HandbookByLaws.aspx)

## Overview

Arkansas runs a hybrid model. The Arkansas Activities Association (AAA) Handbook still treats traditional high school NIL — i.e., a current high school athlete signing a paid endorsement while still in school — as inconsistent with amateur status. However, Arkansas Act 589, signed into law in April 2023, carves out a narrow but powerful exception: once a student-athlete has been admitted to (or signed a National Letter of Intent with) an Arkansas college or university, they may begin earning NIL income before they enroll. For most current high schoolers, this means: you cannot sign a paid deal yet, but you can sign one the moment you have an offer in hand from an in-state college.

## Who can participate

You qualify for the Act 589 exception if you (1) attend an AAA member high school in Arkansas and (2) have been formally admitted to or signed an NLI with an Arkansas college or university. If you have not yet committed to an Arkansas school, you remain bound by AAA amateurism rules and cannot sign paid NIL deals while playing high school sports. Out-of-state college commits are not covered by Act 589 and should treat themselves as ineligible for HS NIL until they enroll in college.

## Before you sign a deal

Verify your college admission or NLI in writing — keep the offer letter or signed NLI on file before you sign anything else. Then read the contract for two specific things: (a) it must not be conditioned on you continuing to play for, transfer to, or remain at any high school (that is a banned recruiting inducement under both AAA and the law); and (b) it must not fall into Act 589's banned categories. If you are under 18, your parent or legal guardian must co-sign any binding contract. The AAA Handbook also restricts any use of school uniforms, equipment, or marks at competition sites.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Confirm your Arkansas college admission/NLI is on file before you sign.** Without it, the Act 589 exception does not apply and AAA amateurism rules treat the deal as a violation.
2. **Notify your athletic director in writing within seven (7) days of signing.** Include the brand name, deal terms, dates, and a copy of the contract. The AAA does not specify a uniform statewide deadline, so we use a conservative 7-day window — many schools require faster.
3. **Have a parent or legal guardian co-sign the contract if you are under 18.** Required by Arkansas contract law for minors.
4. **Avoid school uniforms, school logos, school marks, and school facilities in any deal-related content.** AAA bylaws explicitly prohibit using apparel or equipment at competition sites for promotional purposes.
5. **Keep the signed contract, the college acceptance letter, and your disclosure email together for four years.**

## Banned deal categories

Act 589 lists prohibited categories explicitly: adult entertainment, alcohol, gambling, tobacco, marijuana, and any product or service prohibited by the athlete's college conference or governing body (e.g., NCAA-prohibited categories). The state legislature included these to prevent minors from being associated with vice products and to keep the law aligned with NCAA rules so commits don't lose college eligibility. Any deal that conditions payment on enrollment or continued play at a specific high school is also categorically banned.

## Penalties for non-compliance

A student-athlete who signs a deal without meeting Act 589's conditions — or who signs in a banned category — risks loss of AAA eligibility for up to 365 days, return of compensation, and possible loss of college NIL eligibility under NCAA rules. Failure to disclose is itself a violation. Schools that knew of and permitted a violation can face forfeitures.
