# Wisconsin — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions (unusually strict on agents and school-affiliated facilitation)
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** WIAA Constitution, Article VII (Amateur Status & NIL), effective May 2025 — [link](https://www.wiaawi.org/Portals/0/PDF/Eligibility/NIL/NIL-Q-A-forWIAA-AD.pdf)

## Overview

Wisconsin allows high school NIL under a Wisconsin Interscholastic Athletic Association (WIAA) rule the Board of Control approved on April 25, 2025, with the rule taking effect on **publication of the WIAA Bulletin in late May 2025**. Wisconsin's framework is one of the strictest in the country in two specific ways: **(1) you cannot use an agent or any professional representation,** and **(2) anyone associated with your school — including parents of your teammates — cannot fund, broker, or facilitate your deals.** Read those two points carefully — they trip up athletes who assume HS NIL works the same way college NIL does.

## Who can participate

Any WIAA student-athlete in good standing. There is no age floor. If you are a minor, your parent or legal guardian must sign your contract under Wisconsin contract law. **Your own parent can sign your contract — but the parent of a teammate cannot fund or facilitate a deal for you.** That distinction matters.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm five things:

1. **No agent or professional representation.** You cannot retain an agent, marketing rep, or professional negotiator for the deal. Schools also may not recommend agents to you.
2. **No school-affiliated funder or facilitator.** Coaches, school staff, volunteers, boosters, alumni, current teammates, **parents of other student-athletes**, former coaches/staff during your tenure in the district, and any person/business/organization that has contributed financially or materially to a school may not contribute to or facilitate your deal.
3. **No school identification.** No school team uniform, no school marks/logos, no conference or WIAA reference, no school facility in the deliverable.
4. **No pay-for-play.** Compensation cannot be tied to athletic performance, transfer, or remaining at a school.
5. **No banned category** (see below).

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Day 0 — sign the contract yourself, with parental consent.** No agent, no professional rep.
2. **Day 0 — verify the creative.** No uniform, no logos, no facility shoot, no conference/WIAA reference.
3. **Verify the funder.** Run NILPro's WIAA "associated person" check on the source of payment to confirm it is not a school-connected party.
4. **Notify your AD.** WIAA's rule does not mandate a specific disclosure window, but schools are expected to investigate potential violations when they become aware of them. Best practice: notify your AD within 7 days using NILPro's WIAA disclosure template, sent from your own email.
5. **Keep records.** Contract, parental consent, AD notification, funder verification, every deliverable.

## Banned deal categories

- **Agent / professional representation** — flat prohibition.
- **School-affiliated funders and facilitators** — including teammates' parents, boosters, alumni, school donors, and former coaches/staff during your tenure.
- **Gaming and gambling.**
- **Alcohol.**
- **Tobacco, cannabis, and related products.**
- **Banned or illegal substances.**
- **Adult entertainment.**
- **Weapons.**
- **School uniforms, school logos, conference logos, WIAA marks** in any deliverable.
- **Pay-for-play.**
- **Recruiting inducements.**
- **Activities that conflict with academic or team obligations.**

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation of the NIL rule or amateur status rule can result in **ineligibility for further athletic participation** under WIAA. Specific penalty timelines and reinstatement procedures follow WIAA's standard eligibility-rule enforcement, with school self-reporting and case-by-case review.
