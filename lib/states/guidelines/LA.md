# Louisiana — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** LHSAA Executive Committee Statement on NIL (April 7, 2022) — [link](https://www.lhsaa.org/)

## Overview

Louisiana permits high school NIL. On April 7, 2022 the Louisiana High School Athletic Association (LHSAA) Executive Committee issued a statement clarifying that LHSAA bylaws do not prohibit student-athletes from signing NIL deals, provided amateur status is preserved. The LHSAA's stated approach is **"education, not regulation"** — the burden is on you and your family to keep deals compliant. LHSAA partners with Eccker Sports for state-wide NIL education and requires school ADs and principals to complete the NIL Playbook Course.

## Who can participate

Any LHSAA student-athlete in good standing may sign NIL deals. There is no class, age, or sport restriction. The compensation must come to you as an individual — not through your team, your school, or a booster club.

## Before you sign a deal

- **Read the contract with a parent or guardian.** A minor's NIL contract requires parent/guardian co-signature in Louisiana.
- **Confirm amateurism is preserved.** Compensation cannot be tied to athletic performance, and the deal cannot exist as a recruiting inducement.
- **Strip school identity.** No school name, logo, uniform, or mascot in the deliverables.
- **Check the category.** LHSAA's vice-industry restrictions block tobacco, gambling, and similar categories.
- **Talk to your AD.** Louisiana's "education, not regulation" approach assumes you are using your school's NIL education resources. ADs and principals are required to complete the NIL Playbook Course — use them.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Notify your athletic director in writing.** LHSAA does not publish a fixed disclosure deadline, but written notice to your AD before the deal goes live is the safe practice and protects you under Louisiana's education-first model.
2. **Get parent/guardian co-signature.** Required for minors.
3. **Avoid LHSAA and school marks.** No school logos, mascots, uniforms, or LHSAA logos.
4. **Don't use school facilities.** No filming on campus.
5. **Don't accept school-funded compensation.** No money from the school, booster club, foundation, or coaches.
6. **Document everything.** Contract, payments, every post, and your AD notification.
7. **Confirm collegiate-eligibility impact yourself.** Check NCAA, NJCAA, and NAIA rules separately.

## Banned deal categories

LHSAA's policy and Louisiana school district policies block NIL activity tied to:

- **Tobacco products.**
- **Gambling and sports betting.**
- **Alcohol.**
- **Cannabis and CBD products.**
- **Adult entertainment.**
- **Weapons and firearms.**
- **Banned or illegal substances.**

Also prohibited:

- **Performance-contingent pay.** Stats-based bonuses violate amateurism.
- **Recruiting inducements.** Deals cannot exist to bring you to or hold you at a school.
- **School-driven compensation.** No money sourced from the school, boosters, foundations, or staff.
- **Use of school or LHSAA intellectual property.**

## Penalties for non-compliance

LHSAA can declare a student-athlete ineligible for violations of amateurism or NIL guidelines. Restoration is case-by-case. Because Louisiana's model relies heavily on athlete-and-family responsibility, "I didn't know" is rarely accepted as a defense once you have used school NIL education resources.
