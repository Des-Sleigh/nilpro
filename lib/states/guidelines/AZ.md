# Arizona — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** AIA Bylaw 15.11 (Article 15, Student Eligibility — Amateurism / NIL) — [link](https://aiaonline.org/files/65/article-15-student-eligibility-rules.pdf)

## Overview

Arizona high school athletes can earn money from their name, image, and likeness under a clarified bylaw the Arizona Interscholastic Association (AIA) Legislative Council adopted in early 2024 and incorporated into its 2025-26 student eligibility rules. The provision sits in Article 15 of the AIA Bylaws as Bylaw 15.11. The rule allows NIL only when the activity is fully separate from your AIA-sanctioned team or program, contains no school marks, takes place off school facilities, and is reported to your athletic director within five school days.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete at an AIA member high school may participate, across all sanctioned sports and grade levels. The bylaw does not impose a minimum age, but if you are under 18, Arizona contract law requires a parent or legal guardian to co-sign any binding contract. Middle-school athletes whose schools fall outside AIA jurisdiction are not covered by this rule and should ask their school district for guidance.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm three things before you sign anything: (1) the deal is not an inducement to enroll or stay at a specific school — that alone makes it illegal under AIA rules; (2) the brand is not a "booster," collective, or anyone acting as an agent for your school or athletic program; (3) the contract uses no school marks (school name, district name, logo, uniforms, mascots, AIA logos). Read the deliverables. If the brand wants you in your jersey on the field, that is a banned mark and a banned facility — not negotiable. If you are a minor, plan for a parent to co-sign.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Notify your athletic director within five (5) school days of signing.** AIA Bylaw 15.11 specifically requires that you (or your parent/guardian) notify your school's AD within five school days of entering into any NIL contract or agreement. Email is acceptable unless your school requires its own form.
2. **Have a parent or legal guardian co-sign if you are under 18.** Arizona law treats unsigned-by-guardian contracts with minors as voidable.
3. **Use no school marks anywhere in deal-related content.** That includes uniforms, district name, logos, mascots, or AIA-branded gear. No exceptions, including for posed photos or cropped images.
4. **Do not use AIA member-school facilities for the deal.** No filming or photo shoots at your school's field, gym, weight room, locker room, or parking lot.
5. **Keep your signed contract and the disclosure email for at least three years.** The AIA may audit. If you change schools, re-disclose to the new athletic director on day one.

## Banned deal categories

The AIA bans deals that act as enrollment inducements (the "recruiting firewall") because they would distort competitive balance among schools. It bans deals from school-affiliated boosters and collectives for the same reason. It bans use of school marks and facilities to protect district intellectual property and prevent the appearance of school endorsement. Athletes are also expected to avoid product categories restricted to minors under Arizona law: alcohol, tobacco, vaping, cannabis, controlled substances, gambling, adult entertainment, and firearms.

## Penalties for non-compliance

The AIA can declare an athlete ineligible — typically for the remainder of the season in the sport where the violation occurred, with longer suspensions possible for repeat offenses. The athlete may be required to return any compensation received from the non-compliant deal. Schools can face forfeitures and additional sanctions if officials were aware. A late or missed disclosure (after the five-school-day window) is itself a violation.
