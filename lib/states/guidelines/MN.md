# Minnesota — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** MSHSL NIL Policy & Bylaw 201.00 (Amateur Status) — [link](https://www.mshsl.org/sites/default/files/2022-06/9.D_NIL%20Statement%20Draft.pdf)

## Overview

Minnesota high school athletes can earn money from name, image, and likeness deals under the Minnesota State High School League's NIL Policy, adopted by the MSHSL Board of Directors in June 2022. The policy lives alongside Bylaw 201.00, which defines amateur status for League activities. Minnesota's framework follows the standard "no school identity, no vice industries, no school-time promotion" model used across most permitting states.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete enrolled at an MSHSL member school who is otherwise eligible. The policy applies regardless of sport. There is no carve-out for any specific sport or grade level — if you are competing in MSHSL-sponsored athletics, these rules apply to you.

## Before you sign a deal

- If you are under 18, obtain written parental or guardian consent.
- Confirm the deal does not feature MSHSL or school marks, logos, mascots, or uniforms.
- Confirm the deal does not require you to perform NIL activity during a required team event, practice, contest, travel, or other school-related obligation.
- Confirm the brand is not in a banned category (see below).
- Make sure the deal is not framed as recruiting compensation or as inducement to attend any school.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. Sign and keep a copy of the contract. Disclosure to your school's athletic director is best practice and may be required by your district even where MSHSL does not mandate it centrally.
2. Do not reference MSHSL, your school, or any school activity in promotional content. The mention rule includes verbal references, captions, and visible team gear.
3. Do not miss practice, competition, travel, or team obligations to fulfill an NIL opportunity.
4. Autograph deals are allowed for compensation, but you cannot use school marks, and you cannot do paid autographs during required team activities or other school events.
5. Track income for taxes. NIL is taxable income.

## Banned deal categories

- **Gaming and gambling** — prohibited because it threatens competitive integrity.
- **Alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, and related products** — prohibited per MSHSL chemical-eligibility values.
- **Banned or illegal substances** — performance-enhancing drugs and any substance illegal under federal or Minnesota law.
- **Adult entertainment** — prohibited because athletes are minors.
- **Contraceptive and sexual enhancement products** — explicitly listed in MSHSL policy.
- **Weapons, including firearms** — prohibited.
- **Any deal using MSHSL or school marks, mascots, or logos** — prohibited.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation can result in loss of amateur status under Bylaw 201.00, which makes you ineligible for League-sponsored activities in that sport. Reinstatement requires a formal review by your school and may require MSHSL Board involvement for serious or repeated violations.
