# Maine — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** MPA Handbook, Appendix DD — Name, Image, and Likeness Policy — [link](https://mpaprof.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/handbook.pdf)

## Overview

Maine permits high school NIL. The Maine Principals' Association (MPA) adopted Appendix DD — the Name, Image, and Likeness Policy — on April 28, 2022, and has refined the language in subsequent handbook editions. The rule's distinguishing features are the bright line between "team activities" and NIL activities (no NIL apparel, branding, or promotion during team activity) and a hard ban on any school employee being involved with your deals.

## Who can participate

Any MPA student-athlete may sign NIL deals. There is no class, age, or sport limit. The compensation must come to you as an individual — not through your team, your school, or any school employee.

## Before you sign a deal

- **Read the contract with a parent.** A minor's NIL contract requires parent/guardian co-signature in Maine.
- **Confirm no school employee is involved.** Coaches, teachers, ADs, and administrators are explicitly prohibited from being involved with your NIL — even informally.
- **Strip school and MPA references.** Your deliverables cannot reference your school or the MPA.
- **Plan deliverables outside team activities.** You cannot wear NIL partner apparel, display NIL partner logos, or promote NIL partners during practice, games, or any team activity.
- **Check the category.** Maine's banned categories include adult entertainment, alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, controlled substances, prescription pharmaceuticals, gambling/sports betting, and weapons/firearms.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Notify your athletic director in writing.** MPA does not publish a fixed disclosure window in Appendix DD, but written notice before the deal goes live is the safe practice and protects you if questions are raised later.
2. **Get parent/guardian co-signature.** Required for minors.
3. **Keep school employees out.** No coach, teacher, AD, or administrator may be involved in arranging, negotiating, or executing your deal.
4. **No NIL partner apparel during team activities.** Don't wear sponsor gear or display sponsor logos at practice, games, or any team activity.
5. **No school or MPA references.** Your deliverables cannot reference your school or the MPA.
6. **No promotion during team activities.** Don't post sponsored content while at practice, on the team bus, or at games.
7. **Keep records.** Contract, payments, posts, and AD notification — save everything.

## Banned deal categories

Appendix DD prohibits NIL activity tied to:

- **Adult entertainment.**
- **Alcohol.**
- **Tobacco.**
- **Cannabis products.**
- **Controlled substances and prescription pharmaceuticals.**
- **Gambling, sports betting, lotteries, and video-game betting.**
- **Weapons, firearms, and ammunition.**

Also prohibited:

- **School-employee involvement.** No coach, teacher, AD, or administrator may help with your deal.
- **References to your school or the MPA.**
- **NIL apparel/branding during team activities.**

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation of Appendix DD can result in loss of MPA eligibility. Maine's policy is unusually firm on school-employee involvement — a coach who helps you set up a deal puts both your eligibility and the coach's standing at risk. Restoration is case-by-case.
