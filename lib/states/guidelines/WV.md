# West Virginia — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** WVSSAC Name, Image and Likeness Rule (effective August 8, 2025) — [link](https://www.wvssac.org/rules-regulations/)

## Overview

West Virginia is the newest entrant to high school NIL: the West Virginia Secondary School Activities Commission (WVSSAC) adopted its policy with an effective date of **August 8, 2025**, making West Virginia the 44th state to permit secondary-school NIL. West Virginia is also unique in that it **explicitly covers middle-school athletes** — the rule applies to grades 6 through 12, not just high school. If you compete in a WVSSAC-sanctioned sport in a middle or high school, this policy is your rulebook.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete in **grades 6–12** at a WVSSAC member school in good standing. Middle-school inclusion is a deliberate design choice and reflects how youth athletes — particularly in football, basketball, and wrestling — already build social-media followings before high school. If you are a minor (which you almost certainly are if grades 6–12), your parent or legal guardian must sign your contract under West Virginia contract law.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm the deal does not:

- Reference the WVSSAC or your school in any deliverable.
- Use your school's uniform, mascot, logo, or marks.
- Promote anything from your NIL deal **during team activities** (practices, team meetings, games, team travel).
- Promise NIL opportunity as an inducement to transfer or to attend a member school. Booster clubs, alumni, and collectives connected to a member school are explicitly prohibited from using NIL as a recruiting tool.
- Pay you for athletic performance.

Get a parent or guardian's signature in writing.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Before signing — get parental consent.** Required for all athletes in grades 6–12.
2. **Day 0 — sign the contract.** No school identifiers in the creative.
3. **Notify your principal or AD.** WVSSAC's August 2025 rule emphasizes that schools and school employees may not interfere with or be involved in your NIL deal — but you should still disclose so your school can verify compliance. Use NILPro's disclosure template and send it from your own email within 7 days of signing.
4. **Keep your NIL activity off-team.** No promotion during team time, no team-channel cross-promotion.
5. **Keep records.** Contract, parental consent, principal/AD notification, every deliverable.

## Banned deal categories

- **Drugs, alcohol, weapons.** WVSSAC explicitly bans advertisements for these.
- **School-affiliated content.** No reference to WVSSAC, your school, your team, or your school's marks in any deliverable.
- **Promotion during team activities.** You cannot leverage practice time, team channels, or team travel to push your NIL deal.
- **Recruiting inducements.** Booster clubs, alumni, and collectives associated with a member school cannot use NIL to entice athletes to transfer or enroll.
- **Pay-for-play.**
- **School-facilitated deals.** Coaches, ADs, and school employees cannot broker, negotiate, or otherwise involve themselves in your NIL deal.

## Penalties for non-compliance

WVSSAC enforces the policy through its standard eligibility framework: violations result in loss of eligibility for WVSSAC competition. Schools self-report, WVSSAC reviews, and remedies are determined case-by-case. Because West Virginia's policy is brand-new (less than a year old as of this writing), enforcement precedent is still developing — assume strict reading of the rule rather than betting on lenient enforcement.
