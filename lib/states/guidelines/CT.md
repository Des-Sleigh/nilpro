# Connecticut — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** CIAC Handbook — Amateur Status & NIL Guidelines (Section 4.5.A) — [link](https://www.casciac.org/)

## Overview

The Connecticut Interscholastic Athletic Conference (CIAC) updated its amateur guidelines in June 2022, becoming the 10th state to permit high school NIL. The CIAC permits commercial endorsements, promotional activities, social media monetization, and product/service advertisements — and it explicitly allows representation by an attorney or sports agent. The trade-off: a strict firewall between the personal brand and the school. School logos, uniforms, mascots, and facilities are off-limits, and NIL cannot be used to encourage transfer or persuade an athlete to remain at a particular school.

## Who can participate

All student-athletes at CIAC member high schools may participate, across every sanctioned sport. CIAC's jurisdiction covers grades 9-12. There is no minimum age within the rule, but Connecticut contract law requires a parent or legal guardian to sign any binding contract for an athlete under 18. Athletes may engage attorney or agent representation, but the agent cannot negotiate future intercollegiate or professional contracts on the athlete's behalf while high school eligibility remains.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm the contract has no language tying compensation to your enrollment, transfer, or continued play at any specific school — CIAC's "recruiting firewall" treats that as a categorical violation. Read every deliverable for use of school marks: if you must wear your school's uniform, appear with your team, or shoot at school facilities to satisfy the deal, you cannot do it as written. If you have engaged an agent or attorney, confirm in writing that their representation is limited to NIL and excludes future intercollegiate contracts. If you are under 18, plan for parental co-signature.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Disclose the deal to your athletic director in writing.** CIAC requires NIL agreements be reported to the school. Submit within 7 days of signing as a conservative practice. Include the brand, deal terms, dates, and a copy of the contract.
2. **Have a parent or legal guardian co-sign the contract if you are under 18.** Required by Connecticut contract law for minors.
3. **Avoid school logos, uniforms, mascots, and facilities in any deal-related content.** This includes social media posts. Generic clothing, off-campus settings, no school references is the safe pattern.
4. **Confirm any agent or attorney representation is limited to NIL.** Future intercollegiate contracts are off-limits while you remain a high school athlete.
5. **Keep your signed contract, the disclosure email, and any payment records for at least four years.** The CIAC may audit.

## Banned deal categories

CIAC explicitly prohibits NIL involving adult entertainment, tobacco, cannabis, controlled substances, weapons, and gambling. These categories are banned to keep minors from being commercially associated with products and industries they cannot legally engage with. Additional prohibitions cover: deals tied to athletic performance (which would constitute pay-for-play), deals from professional sports organizations (which would compromise amateur status), and deals that function as enrollment or retention incentives (the recruiting firewall).

## Penalties for non-compliance

A confirmed violation typically results in loss of amateur status and ineligibility in the affected sport, with the duration set by the CIAC. The athlete may be required to return compensation received from the non-compliant deal. Connecticut schools can face additional sanctions — including game forfeitures — if administrators were aware of the violation. Failure to disclose is treated as a violation in itself.
