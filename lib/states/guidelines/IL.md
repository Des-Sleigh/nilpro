# Illinois — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** IHSA By-law 3.084 — Name, Image and Likeness — [link](https://archive.ihsa.org/documents/forms/2025-26/25-26%20School%20Term%201%20Column.pdf)

## Overview

Illinois high school athletes can earn money from NIL under IHSA By-law 3.084, which IHSA member schools approved during the December 2022 referendum. The unusual feature of Illinois's rule is a **time-based restriction**: you cannot do any NIL activity during school hours, while traveling to or from an IHSA event, or during an IHSA event itself. Plan content shoots and posts around your school day and your team's travel.

## Who can participate

Any student in an IHSA member school may participate. There is no grade, age, or sport limit. Compensation must flow to you personally — not to your team, not through your school.

## Before you sign a deal

- **Read the contract with a parent or guardian.** A minor's contract requires parent/guardian co-signature in Illinois.
- **Confirm timing.** If the brand expects content during your school day or during a tournament, that is a violation. Re-scope before signing.
- **Strip school identity.** No IHSA name, logos, or trademarks. No school name, mascot, logo, or trademark.
- **Confirm no facility use.** The brand cannot have you shoot at a member school's facility.
- **Check the category.** Illinois bans gaming/gambling, alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, illegal substances, adult entertainment, and weapons under By-law 3.084.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Notify your athletic director in writing.** IHSA does not set a fixed disclosure deadline in By-law 3.084 itself, but most member schools require notification — confirm your school's rule and send written notice before the deal goes live.
2. **Get parent/guardian co-signature.** Required for minors.
3. **Schedule activities outside school hours and IHSA events.** No shoots, no posts, no appearances during the school day, while traveling to or from an IHSA event, or during the event.
4. **Avoid school facilities.** No filming, photographing, or activations on school grounds.
5. **Avoid IHSA and school marks.** No logos, mascots, uniforms, trademarks, or implied endorsement by IHSA or your school.
6. **Keep records.** Contracts, payments, posts, and AD notifications for the rest of your high school career.
7. **Check NCAA/NJCAA/NAIA impact yourself.** By-law 3.084 puts that responsibility on you.

## Banned deal categories

By-law 3.084 explicitly prohibits NIL activity tied to:

- **Gaming or gambling.** Includes daily fantasy and sportsbook brands.
- **Alcoholic beverages.** Any brand whose product is alcoholic.
- **Tobacco and cannabis.** Including vape and CBD products.
- **Banned or illegal substances.** Performance-enhancing or otherwise prohibited.
- **Adult entertainment products or services.**
- **Weapons.** Firearms, ammunition, related accessories.
- **"Any other product or service the Board deems inappropriate or distracting."** This catch-all is real — when in doubt, ask before you sign.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation of By-law 3.084 can result in loss of IHSA eligibility. Time-based violations (posting during school hours, doing brand work while at an IHSA event) are easy to prove and trigger eligibility review even when the deal itself is otherwise compliant. Restoration is at IHSA's discretion.
