# Idaho — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** IHSAA Rule 8-4-1 — Amateur Status — [link](https://idhsaa.org/rules)

## Overview

Idaho permits high school NIL. The Idaho High School Activities Association (IHSAA) amended Rule 8-4-1 ("Amateur Status") effective for the 2022-2023 school year to allow commercial endorsements, provided **no school, team, league, district, or IHSAA affiliation is involved**. The state has not yet adopted detailed disclosure timelines or vice-category bans like other states, so the burden is on you to keep deals clean.

## Who can participate

Any IHSAA student-athlete in good academic and athletic standing may sign NIL deals. There is no class-year, age, or sport restriction. You sign as an individual — never as a team member, never representing your school.

## Before you sign a deal

- **Read the contract with a parent.** As a minor, your parent or guardian must co-sign for the contract to be valid in Idaho.
- **Strip school identity.** The brand cannot reference your school's name, mascot, or your team. The IHSAA's core rule is no school affiliation.
- **No coach or AD involvement.** A coach forwarding a brand to you can be treated as school affiliation.
- **Verify the deal is for you as a person, not for your team or school.**

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Tell your athletic director.** IHSAA does not currently mandate a specific disclosure window, but written notice to your AD before the deal goes live is the safe practice and protects you if your school later raises questions.
2. **Get parent/guardian signature.** Required for minors.
3. **Avoid IHSAA and school marks.** No IHSAA logo, no school logo, no school colors as a stand-in, no school uniform, no mascot.
4. **Don't film at school.** No school facilities — fields, gyms, locker rooms, weight room — for content shoots.
5. **Don't promote during practice or games.** Even if not in policy text, reasonable separation between athletic activity and brand work is expected.
6. **Keep records.** Save the contract, payments, posts, and AD notification for the duration of your enrollment.

## Banned deal categories

Idaho's adopted rule text is shorter than peer states, but the IHSAA has guidance discouraging the following and your school district likely prohibits them outright:

- **Anything connected to your school.** Marks, logos, facilities, uniforms, coaches, boosters.
- **Performance-based pay.** Compensation tied to stats, points, or wins jeopardizes amateur status.
- **Recruiting inducements.** A deal cannot be designed to bring you to or keep you at a school.
- **Tobacco, alcohol, cannabis, gambling, firearms, adult entertainment, illegal substances.** These are not in Rule 8-4-1's text but every Idaho school district policy prohibits them on campus, and association advisories warn against them.

## Penalties for non-compliance

If a deal compromises your amateur status under Rule 8-4-1, you are ineligible in that sport. The IHSAA handles eligibility issues case-by-case; restoration usually requires terminating the deal, returning compensation, and a formal review. Schools can also be sanctioned for permitting prohibited activity.
