# New Jersey — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** NJSIAA Name, Image and Likeness FAQs (Updated May 2025) — [link](https://www.njsiaa.org/sites/default/files/documents/2022-01/njsiaa-nil-faqs-final.pdf)

## Overview

New Jersey was one of the first states in the country to clear high-school athletes for NIL deals. Effective **January 1, 2022**, the New Jersey State Interscholastic Athletic Association (NJSIAA) amended its amateurism rule to allow you to profit from your own name, image, and likeness, as long as your activities stay completely separated from your school. NJSIAA reviews violations on a case-by-case basis.

## Who can participate

If you are an NJSIAA-eligible student-athlete in good standing — at any public, parochial, or private member school — you are permitted to enter NIL agreements. Eligibility is not tied to grade, sport, or sex. You keep your eligibility as long as your deals follow the rules below.

## Before you sign a deal

- Read the deal end to end. If anything mentions your school, your team, your coaches, NJSIAA, or any league, that language has to come out before you sign.
- Confirm the brand and the product category are not on New Jersey's banned list (see below).
- If you are under 18, your parent or legal guardian should review and co-sign with you. NJSIAA does not require a notary, but most brands will.
- If the deal is meaningful in dollar value (most attorneys suggest above $1,000), have a lawyer look at it.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Sign the agreement** off school property and outside of any team activity.
2. **Keep a clean copy** of the executed agreement, including any addendums.
3. **Do not announce or perform the deal** while wearing your school's uniform, jersey, warm-ups, or anything bearing the school's name, mascot, or logo.
4. **Tell your athletic director.** NJSIAA does not require formal disclosure, but member schools commonly require notice and your AD can flag any school-affiliation problems before they cost you eligibility.
5. **Check in before each new season.** If a deal is ongoing, confirm with your AD that nothing has drifted into school-branded territory.

## Banned deal categories

You may not enter NIL agreements involving:

- Adult entertainment products and services
- Alcohol
- Tobacco, e-cigarettes, and nicotine products
- Cannabis
- Controlled dangerous substances
- Prescription pharmaceuticals
- Casinos, gambling, sports betting, the lottery, and game-based wagering
- Weapons, firearms, and ammunition

NJSIAA chose these categories because they conflict with state law restricting minors and with member-school student conduct codes.

## Penalties for non-compliance

NJSIAA investigates each reported violation individually. Outcomes range from a written warning, to required deal termination, to **immediate suspension of your eligibility**. School employees, coaches, and administrators are barred from involvement in your NIL — if they get involved, both you and the school can be sanctioned.
