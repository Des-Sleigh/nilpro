# New Mexico — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** NMAA Handbook — Section VI, Regulation 6.18.3 — [link](https://www.nmact.org/nmaa-handbook/)

## Overview

The New Mexico Activities Association (NMAA) Board of Directors approved its NIL amendment on **June 1, 2023**, effective for the 2023–24 school year. Regulation 6.18.3 says, plainly: you keep your eligibility while monetizing your name, image, or likeness as long as nothing about the deal implies your school team, school, NMAA, or section is part of it.

## Who can participate

Any NMAA-eligible student-athlete at a member middle school or high school can pursue NIL. There is no separate sign-up. Your eligibility is preserved automatically as long as each deal follows the rules below.

## Before you sign a deal

- Reread the proposed contract and remove any reference to your school's name, logo, mascot, uniform, NMAA, or your section.
- Confirm no coach, school employee, booster, or person acting for your school helped arrange the deal — that alone is a violation.
- Make sure the compensation is for your personal brand and notoriety, not for athletic performance. "Pay for fame" is allowed; "pay for play" is not.
- If you are under 18, have your parent or legal guardian review and co-sign.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Sign on your own time, off school property,** and never during team activities, practices, or competitions.
2. **Keep a copy of the signed agreement** — NMAA strongly encourages you to disclose your activity to your athletic director, even though formal filing is not required.
3. **Do not wear school apparel, uniforms, or training gear** in any commercial photo, video, or appearance.
4. **Do not use facilities, equipment, or marks** belonging to your school for any NIL content.
5. **Loop in your AD** before publishing or going live — they can spot drift toward a school-affiliation issue early.

## Banned deal categories

NMAA's rule does not list a specific federal-style banned-category schedule, but you cannot accept NIL compensation that:

- Is conditioned on athletic performance ("score 20 = $500" deals are out)
- Comes from your school, a school employee, or a booster
- Is used to influence where you enroll or whether you stay enrolled
- Conflicts with your school district's local conduct or wellness policies (most districts ban tobacco, alcohol, gambling, cannabis, firearms, and adult content)

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation can cost you your NMAA eligibility. NMAA reviews each case individually. Schools whose employees or boosters are involved face their own discipline.
