# Delaware — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** DIAA Regulations (Amateurism / NIL — adopted April 2024, effective 2024-25 school year) — [link](https://education.delaware.gov/diaa/)

## Overview

The Delaware Interscholastic Athletic Association (DIAA) Board of Directors approved high school NIL in April 2024, with rules taking effect at the start of the 2024-25 school year. The DIAA framework allows student-athletes to earn compensation from their NIL provided the deal is not contingent on athletic performance, is not an enrollment incentive, is not provided by the school or a school agent, and stays clear of school marks and facilities. The rule applies to every DIAA member school and every sanctioned sport.

## Who can participate

All student-athletes at DIAA member high schools are eligible to participate. The rule applies across grade levels and sports under DIAA jurisdiction. There is no minimum age in the regulation itself, but Delaware contract law requires a parent or legal guardian to sign any binding contract on behalf of a minor (under 18). Athletes who sign with an agent must adhere to the disclosure rule below.

## Before you sign a deal

Read the contract for four red flags: (1) compensation tied to athletic performance or achievement (banned — converts NIL into pay-for-play); (2) the deal is offered as a reason to enroll, stay at, or transfer to a school (banned — recruiting inducement); (3) the deal is from your school, a school employee, a booster, or anyone acting as an agent for the school (banned); (4) the deliverables require school marks, school apparel, or use of school facilities (banned). Confirm none of these apply. If you are under 18, plan for parental co-signature.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Disclose to your athletic director within 72 hours of signing — or before your next scheduled athletic event, whichever comes first.** DIAA's rule applies specifically to agency contracts, but NILPro recommends applying the same 72-hour standard to all NIL deals as a safe practice. Include the brand, deal terms, dates, and a copy of the contract.
2. **Have a parent or legal guardian co-sign the contract if you are under 18.** Required by Delaware contract law for minors.
3. **Use no school marks anywhere in deal-related content.** That means no member school logos, school name, school mascot, no DIAA logos or acronyms, and no school apparel or equipment displaying any of the above.
4. **Do not film or photograph deal-related content at any DIAA member-school facility.** Locker rooms, fields, gyms, and weight rooms are all off-limits for NIL.
5. **Save your signed contract, parent co-signature, and disclosure email for at least four years.** The DIAA may request documentation if an issue arises.

## Banned deal categories

DIAA explicitly bans NIL deals endorsing tobacco, alcohol, and gambling — categories restricted to minors under Delaware law and historically associated with industries the state believes incompatible with school athletics. NILPro recommends athletes also avoid vaping and nicotine products, cannabis and controlled substances, adult entertainment, firearms, and prescription pharmaceuticals; these are widely banned in other state HS NIL frameworks and may be added to the DIAA list over time. Performance-based pay, school-source pay, and enrollment-incentive deals are categorically prohibited.

## Penalties for non-compliance

A confirmed violation results in loss of amateur status and ineligibility in the sport in which the violation occurred. The DIAA can require the athlete to return compensation tied to the non-compliant deal, and schools that knew of the violation can face additional sanctions including forfeitures. Failure to disclose within the 72-hour window is itself a violation, even if every other element of the deal was compliant.
