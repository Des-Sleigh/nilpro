# Rhode Island — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** RIIL Rules and Regulations — Article I, Section 19 (NIL / Amateur Status) — [link](https://www.riil.org/page/6982)

## Overview

The Rhode Island Interscholastic League (RIIL) permits NIL monetization under **Article I, Section 19** of its Rules and Regulations. NIL activity does not jeopardize your amateur status as long as you keep your deals separate from your RIIL school's identity and outside RIIL-sanctioned events. Rhode Island has been on the list of NIL-permitting states since the early state-by-state wave.

## Who can participate

Any RIIL-eligible student-athlete at a member middle school or high school may pursue NIL. There is no separate registration with the league. Eligibility holds as long as each deal follows the rules below.

## Before you sign a deal

- Reread the contract and remove every reference to RIIL, your member school, school name, mascot, uniform, or logo.
- Confirm no coach, school employee, or booster facilitated the deal.
- Confirm compensation is **not** tied to athletic performance, statistics, or playing time — pay-for-play is forbidden.
- If you are under 18, have your parent or legal guardian review and co-sign.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Sign off school property** and outside team activity.
2. **Keep a signed copy** of the agreement.
3. **Notify your athletic director.** RIIL does not require formal filing at the league level, but member schools commonly require notice; your AD is your first eligibility safeguard.
4. **Do not wear school apparel or your school uniform** in any branded content, photo, or video.
5. **Do not wear branded NIL gear at RIIL-sanctioned events** (such as state and class championships). Footwear is generally the only branded item permitted at RIIL events; everything else must be school-issued at official competitions. Branded gear can be worn at non-RIIL events.
6. **Do not use school facilities** for NIL shoots or appearances.
7. **Re-confirm each season** that nothing has drifted into school-branded territory or booster involvement.

## Banned deal categories

RIIL's rule does not publish a federal-style banned-product schedule, but you may not enter NIL agreements that:

- Are conditioned on athletic performance (pay-for-play)
- Use RIIL or member-school marks, uniforms, mascots, or facilities
- Are arranged or funded by a school employee, coach, or booster
- Are used as an inducement to enroll or remain at a particular school
- Conflict with your school district's local conduct or wellness policies — most Rhode Island districts ban tobacco, alcohol, cannabis, gambling, firearms, controlled substances, and adult entertainment

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation may result in **loss of RIIL eligibility**. Wearing branded apparel at RIIL-sanctioned events (other than allowed footwear) can also trigger event-level discipline. RIIL reviews each case on its facts.
