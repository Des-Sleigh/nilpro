# Massachusetts — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** MIAA Handbook (Rule 47 — Amateur Status & NIL) — [link](https://www.miaa.net/sites/default/files/2025-06/miaa-handbook-25-27.pdf)

## Overview

Massachusetts high school athletes can earn money from name, image, and likeness deals under the Massachusetts Interscholastic Athletic Association (MIAA) Handbook. The relevant provisions live in Rule 47 (Amateur Status), most recently updated in the 2025-2027 Handbook cycle. MIAA's framework lets you monetize your personal brand as long as you keep school identity completely out of the deal and disclose every contract to your school.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete attending an MIAA member school who is otherwise eligible. Both public and private MIAA members are bound by the same NIL rules. Middle school athletes participating in MIAA-sanctioned competition follow the same standards.

## Before you sign a deal

- If you are under 18, obtain written parental or guardian consent before signing.
- Read the contract carefully. Confirm there is no use of school name, logo, mascot, nickname, or any MIAA marks.
- Confirm the brand is not in a banned category (see below).
- Decide whether you want representation. If you hire an agent or attorney, that representation agreement is also disclosable to your school.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. Within a reasonable time after signing, provide your school (athletic director or principal) with a copy of every endorsement contract, employment agreement, or representation agreement. MIAA requires schools to maintain copies on file.
2. Do not wear school uniforms or display any school marks in promotional content, photos, or video.
3. Do not perform NIL activity — including wearing sponsor logos or filming sponsored content — during team practices, games, MIAA tournament events, or team travel.
4. Keep your training schedule and academic obligations primary. NIL cannot displace team commitments.
5. Track your income for tax purposes.

## Banned deal categories

- **Alcohol, tobacco, nicotine, vaping, and cannabis** — disallowed because athletes are minors and these conflict with MIAA's wellness mission.
- **Sports betting, gambling, and casinos** — prohibited; threatens competitive integrity.
- **Adult entertainment / sexually explicit products** — prohibited.
- **Firearms, weapons, and ammunition** — prohibited.
- **Performance-enhancing substances or supplements containing banned ingredients** — prohibited.
- **Any deal using school marks, uniforms, or facilities** — prohibited even if the underlying product is otherwise fine.

## Penalties for non-compliance

A student-athlete who violates Rule 47 is suspended from all interscholastic athletics. To return to play, you must apply to the MIAA Eligibility Review Board for reinstatement of amateur status — the process is not automatic and the Board reviews each case individually.
