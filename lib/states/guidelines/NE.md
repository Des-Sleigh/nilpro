# Nebraska — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** NSAA Constitution & Bylaws — Bylaw 3.7.1 (NIL) — [link](https://nsaahome.org/constitution-bylaws/)

## Overview

Nebraska high school athletes can earn money from name, image, and likeness deals under the Nebraska School Activities Association (NSAA) Bylaw 3.7.1, adopted by the NSAA Board of Directors in December 2021 and updated in subsequent cycles. Nebraska was an early permitting state and follows the standard "individual basis, no school connection" model. NIL must be conducted as your individual personal brand — not as a representative of your school or the NSAA.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete enrolled at an NSAA member school who is otherwise eligible to compete in NSAA activities. The rule applies to all sanctioned sports.

## Before you sign a deal

- Get written parental or guardian consent if you are under 18.
- Confirm the deal does not use, suggest, or imply endorsement or sponsorship by your school or the NSAA.
- Confirm no school or NSAA logo, mascot, uniform, or branded gear appears in any photo, video, or text.
- Confirm the brand is not in a banned category (see below).
- Confirm you are signing as an individual, not as a member of a team or as part of a group of teammates.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. Sign in your own name. The NSAA framework is built around individual NIL — group deals with multiple teammates are not permitted under Bylaw 3.7.1.
2. Provide a copy of the contract to your school's athletic director. Schools generally maintain a local file even where NSAA does not require central submission.
3. Do not appear in your school uniform, school-issued warmups, or any clothing or gear depicting your school's name or logo in promotional content.
4. Do not promote during NSAA practices, contests, tournaments, or team travel.
5. Keep all payment records for tax purposes — NIL income is taxable.
6. If a deal is renewed or extended, update your school's file accordingly.

## Banned deal categories

- **Alcohol, tobacco, vaping, and cannabis** — prohibited under NSAA wellness standards.
- **Sports betting, gambling apps, and casinos** — prohibited; threatens competitive integrity.
- **Adult entertainment** — prohibited because athletes are minors.
- **Firearms, weapons, and ammunition** — prohibited.
- **Performance-enhancing substances** — prohibited.
- **Any deal that suggests or reasonably suggests school or NSAA endorsement** — prohibited even if the underlying brand is fine.
- **Pay-for-play arrangements or recruiting inducements** — prohibited.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation of Bylaw 3.7.1 may result in a determination by your member school and the NSAA that you are ineligible for activity participation. The decision rests jointly with the school and NSAA, with appeals through standard NSAA eligibility processes.
