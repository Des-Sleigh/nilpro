# Texas — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions (the narrowest HS NIL window in the country)
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** UIL NIL Guidance, 2025–26 Policy Information (HB 126 implementation, updated July 15, 2025) — [link](https://www.uiltexas.org/policy/2025-26-policy-info/nil-information)

## Overview

**Read this carefully — Texas is not a general high school NIL state.** The University Interscholastic League (UIL) does **not** allow Texas high school athletes to sign brand, collective, or individual NIL deals while they have remaining UIL eligibility. The only NIL agreements a Texas high school athlete may sign are with **postsecondary institutions (colleges and universities)**, and only if the athlete is **17 or older** — and even then, payment is **deferred** until the athlete enrolls in college. Every other NIL deal you might be offered as a Texas high school athlete is prohibited.

This framework was implemented under House Bill 126, signed in June 2025, with UIL guidance updated July 15, 2025.

## Who can participate

You can sign **only** a college NIL agreement, and **only** if all of these are true:

- You are **17 years old or older.**
- You are a **prospective student-athlete** at a postsecondary institution.
- The agreement is **between you and a college or university** — not a brand, agency, collective, individual donor, or booster.
- **No money or in-kind value is paid to you, your family, or anyone on your behalf** until you enroll at the postsecondary institution.

**If you are 16 or younger, you cannot sign any NIL agreement — period.** Family members (by blood or marriage) also cannot execute NIL deals on your behalf.

Any NIL agreement with an entity that is **not** a postsecondary institution may not be executed until you have **exhausted UIL eligibility** in that sport.

## Before you sign a deal

The single most important question: **is the counterparty a university?** If it is anything else — a brand, an agency, a sneaker company, a local business, a NIL collective, an individual donor, a parent's employer — you cannot sign while you have UIL eligibility remaining.

For permitted college deals:

- Confirm you're 17+.
- Read the payment-deferral language carefully — payment must not start until enrollment.
- Confirm there is no clause that pays a parent, sibling, or third party in your stead during HS.
- Get a parent or guardian's signature.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Day 0 — sign the college NIL agreement.** Parent or guardian co-signs.
2. **Within 5 days — notify UIL and your school.** Texas guidance directs prompt disclosure; use NILPro's UIL disclosure template and submit it yourself within 5 days of execution.
3. **Confirm payment deferral.** Verify in writing that no payment, gift, or in-kind value will be transferred to you or your family before college enrollment.
4. **Keep records.** Contract, parental consent, UIL notice, school notice. Hold them through your final UIL competition.
5. **Don't accept anything else.** Don't post sponsored content, don't accept free product as compensation, don't cash a paycheck from a brand. Any of those ends your UIL eligibility.

## Banned deal categories

- **All brand and individual deals** during HS eligibility (apparel, food, social-media sponsorships, autograph signings, paid posts).
- **All collective deals.**
- **All booster-club and donor-funded deals.**
- **Any NIL payment received before college enrollment**, even from a college you've signed with.
- **Recruiting inducements** for high school transfers.
- **Pay-for-play.**

If you are under 17, **everything** is banned.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Loss of UIL eligibility. UIL applies its standard amateur-rule and benefit-rule penalties, which can include suspension, ineligibility for the sport in which the violation occurred, forfeiture of contests, and team-level sanctions. Receiving NIL compensation outside the narrow college-deferred channel is treated as accepting an impermissible benefit and ends amateur status under UIL rules.
