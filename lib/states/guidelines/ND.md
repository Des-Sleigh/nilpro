# North Dakota — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** NDHSAA Constitution & By-Laws — Amateur Status / NIL Amendment (adopted June 7, 2022) — [link](https://ndhsaa.com/administrators-coaches/eligibility)

## Overview

The North Dakota High School Activities Association (NDHSAA) Board adopted its NIL amendment on **June 7, 2022**, effective for the 2022–23 school year. The amendment confirms that earning money from your name, image, or likeness does not jeopardize your amateur status in NDHSAA athletics — provided you keep deals separated from your school and the deal is not tied to performance or recruitment.

## Who can participate

Any NDHSAA-eligible student-athlete in a member school may pursue NIL. There is no roster, application, or pre-approval process at the association level. Eligibility stays intact as long as each deal complies with the rules below.

## Before you sign a deal

- Reread the contract for any reference to your school name, logo, uniform, league, or NDHSAA. Strike each one before signing.
- Confirm no coach, school employee, or booster set up or facilitated the deal.
- Confirm the compensation is at fair market value for your personal brand and is **not** tied to game performance, statistics, or playing time.
- If you are under 18, have your parent or legal guardian review and co-sign.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Sign off school property** and outside any team practice, contest, or activity.
2. **Keep a complete copy** of the executed agreement.
3. **Notify your athletic director.** NDHSAA does not require a formal filing, but the AD is your school's first eligibility safeguard and your point of contact if anything is questioned.
4. **Do not wear school apparel, uniforms, training gear, or anything with the NDHSAA logo or your school's mascot** in branded content.
5. **Do not use school facilities** (gym, field, weight room, pool) for shoots, appearances, or live commercial activity.
6. **Re-check ongoing deals each season** to confirm nothing has drifted into school-branded territory or booster involvement.

## Banned deal categories

NDHSAA's rule does not publish a federal-style banned-product schedule, but you may not enter NIL deals that:

- Are conditioned on athletic performance (pay-for-play)
- Use school uniforms, logos, marks, or NDHSAA logos
- Are arranged or funded by a booster club or school employee
- Are used as an inducement to attend or remain at a particular school
- Conflict with your school district's local conduct or wellness policies (most districts ban alcohol, tobacco, gambling, cannabis, firearms, and adult content)

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation may result in **loss of NDHSAA eligibility**. NDHSAA reviews each case on its facts. Schools whose employees or boosters became involved face separate discipline.
