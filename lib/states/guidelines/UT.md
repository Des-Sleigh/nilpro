# Utah — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** UHSAA Handbook 2025–26, Section 6 (Amateur Rule) — [link](https://uhsaa.org/Publications/Handbook/Handbook.pdf)

## Overview

Utah has allowed high school NIL since the Utah High School Activities Association (UHSAA) approved a rule update in January 2022, with implementation in the 2022–23 handbook. The current 2025–26 handbook continues the same framework: you may accept money for the use of your name, image, and likeness, but you may not do so in a way that ties the deal to your school identity. The Amateur Rule is the controlling provision.

## Who can participate

Any UHSAA student-athlete in good standing. There is no age floor. If you are a minor, your parent or legal guardian must sign your contract under Utah contract law. Utah's framework is straightforward: protect your athletic individuality, keep your school out of the deal.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm the deal does not require you to:

- Wear a school team uniform.
- Display any school insignia, mascot, logo, wordmark, or colors used by your school for athletic purposes.
- Reference your team affiliation as part of the commercial endorsement (e.g., "[School] starting forward").

If any of those is true, the deal violates the UHSAA Amateur Rule — even if everything else looks fine.

Get parental consent in writing. Read the contract for IP-grant clauses; Utah law treats minors' contracts as voidable, and a sloppy IP grant can outlive your high school career.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Day 0 — sign with parental consent.** The parent/guardian signature is part of basic Utah contract enforceability.
2. **Day 0 — verify the creative.** No school uniform, no school logo, no school facility shoot, no team name in copy.
3. **Notify your AD.** UHSAA's handbook does not impose a fixed disclosure window, but most Utah schools want prompt written notice. Use NILPro's disclosure template and send it from your own account.
4. **Keep records.** Contract, parental consent, AD notification, every published deliverable.
5. **Re-verify before each new campaign.** Renewals and additional deliverables count as new deals for compliance purposes.

## Banned deal categories

- **Anything that puts you in a school uniform or shows school insignia, mascot, name, or marks** in the paid content.
- **Lending your name and team affiliation for commercial endorsement.** Even without a uniform, marketing you as "[School] athlete" violates the Amateur Rule.
- **Pay-for-play.** Compensation tied to athletic performance ends your amateur status.
- **Recruiting inducements.** No deal that exists to entice you to attend or transfer to a particular school.
- **School-arranged or booster-funded deals** that imply school sponsorship.
- **Categories restricted by Utah's general advertising and minor-protection laws**: alcohol, tobacco/vaping, cannabis, gambling, firearms, and adult content — avoid these regardless of UHSAA's silence.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation of the UHSAA Amateur Rule results in loss of amateur status. A student who loses amateur status becomes ineligible for UHSAA competition. Reinstatement is at the UHSAA Executive Committee's discretion and typically requires returning compensation, removing offending content, and serving an ineligibility period.
