# South Dakota — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** SDHSAA Constitution Amendment on Name, Image and Likeness (adopted June 2024) — [link](https://www.sdhsaa.com/Handbook/ATH-GeneralSportsInformation.pdf)

## Overview

South Dakota allows high school NIL under a constitutional amendment the South Dakota High School Activities Association (SDHSAA) adopted in June 2024 after a member-school vote (115 yes / 7 no — 94% approval). The policy lets you build a personal brand and accept NIL compensation, but it draws a hard line between your individual identity as a person and your identity as an SDHSAA athlete. You may use the first; you may not commercialize the second.

## Who can participate

Any student in good standing at an SDHSAA member school. There is no age floor. Your parent or legal guardian must approve any deal you sign while you are a minor — South Dakota contract law requires it, and SDHSAA expects schools to confirm parental consent if a question arises.

## Before you sign a deal

Read the contract carefully and verify the deal is **not** tied to athletic performance ("pay-for-play" is banned), **not** an inducement for you to attend or transfer to a particular school, and **not** structured through your school or a school booster club. SDHSAA member schools are explicitly forbidden from arranging, developing, or promoting a relationship between you and a sponsor.

You also need parental consent in writing before you sign. Keep that consent on file.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Before signing — get parental consent.** Written, dated, signed.
2. **Day 0 — sign the deal.** Do not use SDHSAA marks, school marks, school uniforms, school facilities, or school logos in any aspect of the deliverable.
3. **Within a reasonable time — notify your AD.** SDHSAA does not specify an exact disclosure window in the constitutional amendment, but member schools commonly require disclosure so they can self-report. Use NILPro's disclosure template; send it from your own email to your AD.
4. **Throughout the deal — keep your content clean.** No school jersey, no school facility shoots, no SDHSAA mascot or wordmark.
5. **Save copies of everything.** Contract, parental consent, AD notification, deliverables.

## Banned deal categories

- **Alcohol, tobacco, vaping, controlled substances, gambling, and any banned athletic substances.** SDHSAA explicitly lists these.
- **School/SDHSAA-branded content.** No school logo, no SDHSAA logo, no team uniform, no school facilities in the shoot.
- **Pay-for-play.** Compensation cannot be tied to wins, stats, playing time, or athletic results.
- **Recruiting inducements.** No deal that exists to lure you to a particular school or to keep you from transferring.
- **School-arranged deals.** Your school, coaches, and booster clubs cannot broker, fund, or facilitate.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Loss of amateur status. Under SDHSAA rules, a student who loses amateur status may be reinstated by the SDHSAA Board of Directors only after a one-year lapse. That means a single bad deal can cost you a full season.
