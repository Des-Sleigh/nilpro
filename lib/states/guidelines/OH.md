# Ohio — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions (strict compliance — notarization required)
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** OHSAA Bylaw 4, Section 11 — Name, Image & Likeness / Personal Branding Rights — [link](https://ohsaaweb.blob.core.windows.net/files/Eligibility/NILFINAL.pdf)

## Overview

OHSAA member schools approved Bylaw 4-11 (Personal Branding Rights) by emergency referendum on **November 24, 2025**, making Ohio the 45th state to permit HS NIL. The bylaw is now in effect. Ohio's compliance burden is among the heaviest in the country: **both the student-athlete and a parent or legal guardian must sign a notarized Affirmation page**, and **every agreement must be disclosed via the Personal Branding Rights Parent Portal within 14 days**. Failure to disclose triggers a season-suspension penalty.

## Who can participate

Any OHSAA-eligible student-athlete at a member 7th–12th grade school may pursue NIL after the affirmation and disclosure steps below are completed for each agreement.

## Before you sign a deal

1. **Read Bylaw 4-11-2** and confirm your deal complies with each item (no school marks, no pay-for-play, no booster funding, no school-employee involvement, no banned-category products).
2. **Get the OHSAA Affirmation page.** Both you (the student) and your **parent or legal guardian** must sign it in front of a **notary public**. This is a hard requirement. NILPro produces the form and tracks your due dates, but the notary appointment is on you — banks, UPS Stores, and most public libraries notarize for free or a small fee.
3. **Bring photo ID for both signers** to the notary. Sign the form **in the notary's presence** — not before.
4. **Save the notarized PDF** with your other documents.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

Be precise. The 14-day clock starts the moment the deal is signed.

1. **Day 0 — Sign the agreement** off school property and outside team activity.
2. **Day 0 — Lock in your notary appointment** for the Affirmation page (if not already notarized).
3. **Within 14 days — Submit to the OHSAA Personal Branding Rights Parent Portal**:
   - The complete signed NIL agreement (every page, all exhibits)
   - The notarized Affirmation page (student + parent/guardian)
4. **Wait for the school confirmation email** — your member school typically receives notice within 1–2 business days of submission. Save that confirmation.
5. **Do not perform the deal in school uniform, with school marks, on school facilities, or during team activities.**
6. **Re-disclose any amendment** to the agreement on the same 14-day clock.
7. **Email PersonalBranding@ohsaa.org** if you have any question about whether something needs to be reported. When in doubt, disclose.

## Banned deal categories

You may not enter agreements involving products or services that are illegal for minors or that conflict with OHSAA bylaws, including:

- Pay-for-play arrangements (compensation tied to athletic performance)
- Cash awards over $500 per event (Bylaw 5)
- Adult entertainment
- Alcohol, tobacco, e-cigarettes, cannabis
- Casinos, gambling, sports betting
- Controlled substances and prescription pharmaceuticals
- Firearms, weapons, ammunition
- Use of OHSAA or member-school marks, uniforms, logos, mascots, or facilities

## Penalties for non-compliance

A student who **fails to disclose** an agreement, or fails to disclose **on time** (within 14 days), is subject to a period of ineligibility of **up to 20% of the sport season** in which they participate, plus any other sanctions outlined in Bylaw 11. Falsified information on the Affirmation can independently void eligibility under Bylaw 4-1.
