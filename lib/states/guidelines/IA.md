# Iowa — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** IHSAA & IGHSAU — Name, Image, Likeness Guidance — [link](https://www.iahsaa.org/handbook/name-image-likeness-guidance/)

## Overview

Iowa permits high school NIL. The Iowa High School Athletic Association (IHSAA, boys) and the Iowa Girls High School Athletic Union (IGHSAU) jointly adopted matching NIL guidelines effective for the 2022-2023 school year. The two associations enforce identical standards: deals must be unconnected to your school, your performance, and a defined list of banned categories.

## Who can participate

Any IHSAA or IGHSAU student-athlete in good academic and eligibility standing can sign NIL deals. There is no grade or age cutoff. The compensation must come to you as an individual — not through your school, a coach, a booster club, or a foundation.

## Before you sign a deal

- **Read the contract with a parent or guardian.** As a minor, your parent must co-sign.
- **Confirm the compensation is not tied to performance.** Bonuses for stats, points, or wins violate the rule.
- **Confirm the brand is not connected to your school.** Booster clubs, foundations, coaches, and school employees are off-limits as the source of payment.
- **Check the category.** Iowa explicitly bans gaming/gambling, alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, banned/illegal substances, adult entertainment, and weapons.
- **Talk to your AD before signing.** IHSAA and IGHSAU recommend seeking school guidance and your own legal counsel.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Notify your athletic director in writing.** Iowa's policy strongly recommends school guidance before signing — make written notification before the deal goes live so there is a paper trail.
2. **Get parent/guardian co-signature.** Required for minors.
3. **Avoid IHSAA, IGHSAU, and school marks.** No association logos, no school marks, no mascots, no uniforms.
4. **Don't use school facilities.** No filming or activations on school grounds.
5. **Check NCAA, NJCAA, and NAIA rules.** Iowa's policy specifically tells you to confirm collegiate eligibility implications before signing.
6. **Keep records.** Contract, payments, posts, and AD notification — save it all.

## Banned deal categories

The IHSAA/IGHSAU policy explicitly prohibits NIL activity that promotes:

- **Gaming or gambling.** Includes daily fantasy and sportsbooks.
- **Alcoholic beverages.**
- **Tobacco, cannabis, and related products.** Including vape and CBD.
- **Banned or illegal substances.**
- **Adult entertainment products or services.**
- **Weapons, including firearms.**

The policy also bans:

- **Performance-contingent compensation.** Stats-based pay forfeits amateurism.
- **Recruiting inducements.** Compensation cannot be designed to bring you to or hold you at a school.
- **School-funded compensation.** No money from the school, booster club, or foundation.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violations result in loss of eligibility under IHSAA or IGHSAU rules. Reinstatement is case-by-case and typically requires terminating the deal and returning compensation.
