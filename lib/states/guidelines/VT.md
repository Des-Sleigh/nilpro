# Vermont — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** Vermont Principals' Association (VPA) Athletic Policies — [link](https://vpaonline.org/athletics/faqs-for-athletics/)

## Overview

Vermont allows high school NIL under the Vermont Principals' Association (VPA), which governs interscholastic athletics. The VPA's approach is sometimes described as "protective permissiveness" — it recognizes your individual right to be compensated for your name, image, and likeness, but it draws a sharp line between you-as-a-person and you-as-a-VPA-athlete. Vermont's framework includes a notable carve-out for winter-sports athletes (skiers, snowboarders, hockey players) whose sponsorship economics have always sat outside school athletics.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete attending a VPA member school in good academic and conduct standing. There is no age floor. If you are a minor, your parent or legal guardian must sign your contract under Vermont contract law. The VPA's "non-affiliation" requirement applies to all sports, with a "safe harbor" recognized for winter-sports athletes who sign with local gear retailers, ski resorts, and similar sport-context sponsors.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm the deal does not:

- Reference your school by name, mascot, or wordmark.
- Reference the VPA.
- Use your school's uniform, gear, facilities, or logos.
- Pay you based on athletic performance.
- Function as an inducement to attend or transfer to a particular school.

Verify parental consent is in place — written, signed, and dated — before you put your name on anything. If the deal is with a winter-sports retailer or resort and you compete in those sports, the safe harbor allows for sport-context sponsorship — but not for school-team sponsorship.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Before signing — get parental consent.** Required for minors.
2. **Day 0 — sign the contract.** Confirm separation from school identity in every deliverable.
3. **Notify your principal or AD.** The VPA does not publish a fixed disclosure window in its athletic FAQs, but Vermont member schools commonly expect prompt written disclosure. Use NILPro's disclosure template and send it from your own email.
4. **Verify creative before posting.** Strip school colors, jersey, and facility shots out of any paid content.
5. **Keep records.** Contract, parental consent, principal/AD notification, deliverables.

## Banned deal categories

The VPA's "vice list" prohibits endorsements involving:

- **Alcohol** and alcoholic-beverage promotion.
- **Tobacco, nicotine, vaping** products.
- **Cannabis** (notably banned despite Vermont's legal adult-use market).
- **Gambling** — including the Vermont State Lottery.
- **Firearms and ammunition.**
- **Adult entertainment.**

Plus the structural prohibitions:

- **School-affiliated deals.** No school name, mascot, or VPA reference in promotional material.
- **School uniforms or jerseys** in promotional creative.
- **Performance-based pay.**
- **Recruiting inducements.**

## Penalties for non-compliance

Loss of amateur status under VPA rules, leading to ineligibility for VPA-sanctioned competition. The VPA's athletics handbook addresses reinstatement procedures, which typically require returning compensation and serving an ineligibility period before competition resumes. Specific penalty timelines are determined case-by-case under VPA policy.
