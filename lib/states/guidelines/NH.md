# New Hampshire — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** NHIAA Handbook — Article II (Eligibility / Amateur Status) — [link](https://www.nhiaa.org/about-nhiaa/handbook)

## Overview

New Hampshire high school athletes can earn money from name, image, and likeness deals under the New Hampshire Interscholastic Athletic Association (NHIAA) Handbook. The NHIAA clarified its handbook language in late December 2022 and early 2023, formally permitting commercial endorsements as long as no school, team, or NHIAA affiliation is involved. New Hampshire became one of the early-permitting states and uses a relatively light-touch framework focused on protecting school identity rather than requiring centralized NIL disclosure.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete at an NHIAA member school who is otherwise eligible to compete. The framework applies across all NHIAA-sanctioned sports and grade levels.

## Before you sign a deal

- If you are under 18, obtain written parental or guardian consent.
- Confirm the deal makes no reference to your school team, your school, or the NHIAA — the handbook prohibits capitalizing on athletic fame "in affiliation or connection with" any of those.
- Confirm the deal does not put you in your school uniform or use school or NHIAA marks, logos, or mascots.
- Confirm the brand is not in a banned category (see below).
- Note the prize-money rule: you can accept up to $1,000 in prize money or prizes of equivalent value from competitions without losing amateur status. Above that, you risk eligibility under separate amateur rules.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. Sign as an individual, not on behalf of any team or school.
2. Provide a copy of the contract to your school's athletic director. While NHIAA does not centrally collect every disclosure, schools commonly maintain local files.
3. Do not appear in your school uniform or use any school or NHIAA marks in promotional content. The handbook is explicit that uniform appearances and use of school marks are prohibited.
4. Do not promote during NHIAA practices, contests, tournaments, or team travel.
5. Keep payment records for taxes.

## Banned deal categories

- **Alcohol, tobacco, vaping, and cannabis** — prohibited.
- **Sports betting, gambling, and casinos** — prohibited.
- **Adult entertainment** — prohibited because athletes are minors.
- **Firearms, weapons, and ammunition** — prohibited.
- **Performance-enhancing substances** — prohibited.
- **Any deal using school, team, or NHIAA marks, mascots, or uniforms** — categorically prohibited.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Capitalizing on athletic fame in connection with your school team, school, or NHIAA is an amateur-rule violation, which puts your eligibility at risk. The penalty is loss of amateur status; reinstatement is governed by the NHIAA's eligibility processes.
