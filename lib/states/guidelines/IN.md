# Indiana — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Not permitted (vote pending May 4, 2026)
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** IHSAA By-laws — current amateurism rules — [link](https://www.ihsaa.org/about-us/executive-committee-board-directors)

## Overview

As of today, Indiana is **off**: the Indiana High School Athletic Association (IHSAA) does not permit high school athletes to monetize their name, image, or likeness. The IHSAA Board of Directors votes on Monday, **May 4, 2026** on a "Personal Branding Activity" (PBA) proposal that would change this. **If the PBA proposal passes, this document will be updated.** Until that vote is final and effective, do not sign NIL deals.

## Who can participate

Right now: nobody at the high school level in Indiana. The IHSAA's amateurism rules treat paid endorsement of athletic identity as a forfeiture of amateur status.

If the PBA proposal passes May 4: every IHSAA student-athlete would be eligible, with the rules below as drafted.

## Before you sign a deal

Don't sign. Today, signing a paid NIL deal as an Indiana high school athlete forfeits your eligibility.

If you are talking to a brand:

- Ask them to wait until after the May 4, 2026 vote, and then a follow-up effective date.
- Do not accept money, free product, gifts, travel, or appearance fees in advance of formal IHSAA approval.
- Do not sign anything that "activates upon NIL adoption" — that is still a current contract.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

These obligations apply **only if the PBA proposal passes** and you sign after the effective date:

1. **Notify your athletic director within 48 hours of entering the agreement.** This is the disclosure window in the proposed PBA rule.
2. **Get parent/guardian co-signature.** Required for any minor's contract in Indiana.
3. **Use only your own name — no school references.** As drafted, the PBA proposal allows you to make money under your own name only, with no reference to your school.
4. **Avoid all collectives.** The PBA proposal explicitly prohibits collective involvement.
5. **Allow school review.** Schools may review your PBA agreement to confirm compliance. They cannot block compliant deals.
6. **Keep records.** Save the contract, payments, every post, and your AD notification.

## Banned deal categories (under proposed PBA rule)

If the proposal passes, expect bans consistent with peer states and Indiana school district policies:

- **Collectives.** Strictly prohibited as drafted.
- **School-affiliated deals.** No school name, no school logo, no team reference.
- **Tobacco, alcohol, cannabis, gambling, weapons, adult entertainment, illegal substances.** These are routinely banned in HS NIL rules and conflict with district policies.
- **Performance-contingent pay.** Bonuses tied to stats forfeit amateurism.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Today, signing any paid NIL deal forfeits IHSAA eligibility. Restoration is not automatic.

If the PBA rule passes and you violate it (missed disclosure, school references, collective involvement), expect ineligibility consequences — exact penalty schedule will be set by IHSAA on adoption.
