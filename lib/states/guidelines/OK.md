# Oklahoma — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** OSSAA Guidelines Regarding Name, Image and Likeness (NIL) — [link](https://ossaaillustrated.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/MF_2022-23_NILGuidelines-1.pdf)

## Overview

The Oklahoma Secondary School Activities Association (OSSAA) approved its NIL guidelines in October 2022 and they remain in effect. NIL activity does not put your amateur status at risk so long as it complies with **Rule 5** (amateurism) and the school-affiliation restrictions below. OSSAA partners with Eccker Sports for athlete and family education.

## Who can participate

Any OSSAA-eligible student-athlete at a member school can pursue NIL. There is no separate registration. Eligibility holds as long as each deal follows the requirements below.

## Before you sign a deal

- Reread the contract and remove every reference to your school name, mascot, uniform, logo, OSSAA logos, or OSSAA acronyms.
- Confirm no coach, school employee, or person acting as an agent of the school helped arrange the deal.
- Confirm compensation is not contingent on athletic performance and is not an inducement to enroll or remain at a particular member school.
- Confirm the deal does not conflict with your local school district's policies (commonly: tobacco, alcohol, drugs, gambling, firearms, adult content).
- If you are under 18, have a parent or legal guardian review and co-sign.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Sign off school property** and outside any team activity.
2. **Keep a complete copy** of the executed agreement.
3. **Notify your athletic director.** OSSAA does not require formal filing at the association level, but member schools commonly require notice and your AD is your eligibility safeguard.
4. **Do not wear school apparel or equipment** that includes school name, logo, mascot, or any OSSAA-trademarked logos in any commercial appearance, photo, or video.
5. **Do not use member-school facilities** for any NIL appearance, shoot, or activation.
6. **Do not promote your deal during a school-based team activity** (practice, travel, contest, locker room, etc.).
7. **Re-confirm each season** that nothing has drifted into school-branded territory.

## Banned deal categories

You may not enter NIL deals that:

- Are conditioned on athletic performance, statistics, or playing time (pay-for-play)
- Involve school logos, names, mascots, uniforms, OSSAA logos, or OSSAA acronyms
- Use member-school facilities for NIL compensation
- Conflict with your school district's local policy — most districts prohibit tobacco, alcohol, gambling, cannabis, controlled substances, firearms, and adult entertainment
- Are arranged by a school, school employee, coach, booster, or any agent of the school

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation can result in **loss of OSSAA eligibility** and may trigger separate Rule 5 amateurism review. Schools whose employees or boosters become involved face their own discipline.
