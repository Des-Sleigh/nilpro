# Hawaii — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Not permitted
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** HHSAA Administrative Regulations — Amateur Status — [link](https://hhsaa.org/assets/content/resources/governing_documents/Administrative-Regulations.pdf)

## Overview

Hawaii high school athletes **cannot** monetize their name, image, or likeness without losing eligibility. The Hawaii High School Athletic Association (HHSAA) has not adopted an NIL policy, and Hawaii has not passed an NIL law that overrides HHSAA amateurism rules. If you sign a paid NIL deal while enrolled at an HHSAA member school, you forfeit your amateur status and your eligibility to play.

## Who can participate

No HHSAA student-athlete may participate in paid NIL activity. This applies to every sport, every grade, every classification. The bar is sport-by-sport, but in practice signing any paid endorsement deal is treated as competing for money under HHSAA amateurism rules.

If you are a high school athlete in Hawaii and a brand approaches you with money, the safe answer right now is no.

## Before you sign a deal

Don't sign. Until HHSAA adopts an NIL policy, accepting compensation for your athletic identity will end your high school athletic career.

If a brand wants to work with you:

- Ask them to wait until you graduate.
- Ask whether they are willing to do a non-paid activation that does not reference athletics (no uniform, no team, no sport context, no athletic claims). This area is gray and risky — talk to your AD before agreeing to anything.
- Do not accept gifts, free product, travel, or appearance fees in lieu of cash. HHSAA treats these as compensation.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

You should not be signing a paid NIL deal at all. If you are unsure whether something counts as compensation:

1. **Talk to your athletic director first.** Before any signature, payment, or post.
2. **Get the brand's request in writing.** So your AD has something concrete to evaluate.
3. **Wait for written clearance.** Do not assume verbal "it's fine" is enough.
4. **Document everything.** If you accept anything you believe is non-athletic, save the agreement and AD correspondence.

## Banned deal categories

Effectively all paid endorsement and sponsorship categories are off-limits while you are enrolled at an HHSAA school:

- Brand endorsements
- Paid social posts
- Appearance fees and autograph signings for compensation
- Camp/clinic compensation tied to your athletic identity
- Cash awards from any interscholastic competition
- Group/team licensing deals
- Collective payments

## Penalties for non-compliance

Loss of amateur status under HHSAA rules means **loss of eligibility in that sport**, full stop. Once amateur status is forfeited, restoration is not automatic and typically requires a formal HHSAA review. A violation can also carry forward — colleges look at HHSAA findings during recruiting.
