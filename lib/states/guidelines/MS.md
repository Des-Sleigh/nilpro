# Mississippi — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Not permitted
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** MHSAA Rules & Eligibility (Amateur Standing) — [link](https://www.misshsaa.com/rules/)

## Overview

Mississippi does **not** permit high school NIL. The Mississippi High School Activities Association (MHSAA — Mississippi's governing body for HS sports, not to be confused with Michigan's MHSAA) prohibits student-athletes from monetizing their name, image, or likeness in any way that touches their athletic participation. The most recent legislative attempt to change this — **House Bill 1400 (2026 Regular Session)**, the "Mississippi High School Student-Athlete NIL Protection Act" — died in committee in early 2026. As of April 29, 2026, monetizing your NIL while playing MHSAA-sanctioned sports will cost you your eligibility.

## Who can participate

**Nobody, while remaining eligible for MHSAA athletics.** If you take any payment, product, or service in exchange for your name, image, or likeness, you forfeit your amateur status under MHSAA bylaws and become ineligible to compete. This applies to all sports MHSAA sanctions, all classifications, and all member schools (public and private members alike).

If you do not play MHSAA-sanctioned sports — for example, you only compete in club or AAU events outside the school system — you are not bound by MHSAA rules and may pursue NIL freely. But the moment you suit up for a Mississippi high school team, the prohibition kicks in.

## Before you sign a deal

You should not sign an NIL deal while you are an MHSAA athlete. If you are considering one anyway, understand:

- Signing will end your high school eligibility.
- There is no disclosure pathway, waiver process, or pre-approval mechanism — the prohibition is categorical.
- A deal entered "in secret" is still a violation and will result in retroactive forfeiture of contests if discovered.

If you are a senior who has already signed with a college and your final MHSAA contest is behind you, your post-high-school NIL is governed by the NCAA, NAIA, or NJCAA framework of your destination institution — not by MHSAA. But you cannot collect on an NIL deal while you still have MHSAA games to play.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

Not applicable — deals are not permitted. If you nevertheless sign one, the only "obligation" inside the MHSAA system is to self-report to your school's athletic director and accept the eligibility consequences.

## Banned deal categories

All categories are banned. The prohibition is on the act of receiving NIL compensation, not on specific industries.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Loss of amateur status, which means immediate ineligibility for all MHSAA-sanctioned interscholastic competition. Schools that knowingly permit ineligible athletes to compete may face team forfeitures, fines, and post-season bans.
