# Virginia — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** VHSL Name, Image and Likeness Policy (effective July 1, 2023) — [link](https://www.doe.virginia.gov/parents-students/student-resources/name-image-and-likeness-nil)

## Overview

Virginia allows high school NIL under a Virginia High School League (VHSL) policy that took effect July 1, 2023, after a 32–2 committee vote. Virginia's framework is unusually clean for athletes and parents because the disclosure rule is unambiguous: **you must notify your principal or athletic director in writing within 72 hours of signing any NIL contract.** That 72-hour clock is the clearest in the country and is the single most important obligation to internalize.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete enrolled at a VHSL member school in good standing. There is no age floor. If you are a minor, your parent or legal guardian must sign your contract under Virginia contract law. Schools self-report violations to VHSL, so your AD is your front-line compliance partner — keep that relationship clean and proactive.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm the deal:

- **Does not** reference your school, team, district, region, or the VHSL anywhere in promotional materials.
- **Does not** use your school's uniform, mascot, logo, or trademark.
- **Is not** in a banned category (see below).
- **Is not** structured through a "collective" connected to your school. Schools are barred from organizing collectives and athletes cannot accept payments from one connected to a VHSL member school.
- **Is not** tied to athletic performance ("pay-for-play").

Get parental consent in writing before signing.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Day 0 — sign the contract.** Parent or guardian co-signs if you're a minor.
2. **Day 0 to Day 3 — notify your principal or AD in writing.** This is the **72-hour rule** and it is the hard requirement of the VHSL policy. Use NILPro's VHSL disclosure template; send it from your own email.
3. **Verify creative.** No school name, no jersey, no team logo, no district/region/VHSL reference.
4. **Keep records.** Contract, parental consent, the timestamped 72-hour disclosure email, every deliverable.
5. **Re-disclose for renewals or new deals.** Each new contract restarts the 72-hour clock.

## Banned deal categories

VHSL bans endorsements involving:

- **Adult-themed material.**
- **Tobacco and vaping products.**
- **Casinos and gambling.**
- **Guns and firearms.**
- **Illegal drugs.**

Structural prohibitions:

- **School-affiliated content.** No reference to your school, team, district, region, or VHSL in NIL materials.
- **School-organized collectives.** Schools are prohibited from organizing collectives, and deals routed through one are non-compliant.
- **Pay-for-play.**
- **Recruiting inducements.**

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation results in **VHSL ineligibility.** Schools are responsible for self-reporting violations. Practical impact: a missed 72-hour disclosure or a deal in a banned category can take you out of competition mid-season. VHSL determines reinstatement on a case-by-case basis and typically requires returning compensation, removing offending content, and serving an ineligibility period.
