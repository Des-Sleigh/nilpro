# Alaska — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** ASAA Handbook, Article 8 — Amateurism (NIL provision adopted Dec. 7, 2021) — [link](https://asaa.org/about/handbook/)

## Overview

Alaska was one of the earliest states to formally permit high school NIL. On December 7, 2021, the Alaska School Activities Association (ASAA) Board amended its longstanding amateurism rule (Article 8) to allow student-athletes to participate in commercial endorsements provided there is "no school team, school, ASAA Region, or ASAA affiliation" in the deal. Olympic gold medalist Lydia Jacoby — who signed with swimwear brand Arena while still a high school athlete in Seward — is the most well-known precedent for how the rule works in practice.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete enrolled at an ASAA member school may engage in NIL activity, across all sports and grade levels covered by ASAA. There is no minimum age, but if you are under 18, your contracts will need a parent or legal guardian to sign — Alaska contract law, not the ASAA rule, drives that. The single, dominant rule is the school-separation rule: your NIL activity must be wholly disconnected from your school, team, region, or ASAA itself.

## Before you sign a deal

Read every line of the proposed contract for any reference to your school, team, conference, or ASAA. If the deal references any of those — directly, in photography, or by implication — you must either remove the reference or decline the deal. Confirm with your athletic director that the brand and the activity do not violate the school-separation rule. If you are a minor, your parent or legal guardian must sign the contract with you. Make sure you understand the full obligations: posting frequency, exclusivity, term length, and what happens when you graduate.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Notify your athletic director in writing before you publish any content.** Email is fine. Include the brand name, what you are being paid (cash, product, or both), the deal start and end dates, and a copy of the signed contract.
2. **Have a parent or guardian co-sign if you are under 18.** Required by Alaska contract law for any binding agreement involving a minor.
3. **Strip every school reference from your content.** No school jersey, no team name, no ASAA logos, no filming on school grounds, no mascot. Even a wall poster of your school behind you in a sponsored video can violate the rule.
4. **Do not use school facilities — fields, weight rooms, gyms — for any deal-related content.** Shoot at home or on public ground.
5. **Keep a signed copy of the contract and any payment records for at least four years.** ASAA may request documentation if a question arises.

## Banned deal categories

ASAA's rule is structured around school separation rather than a list of "vice" categories, but you are still bound by the law where you live: no deals involving alcohol, tobacco, vaping or nicotine products, cannabis, controlled substances, gambling or sports betting, adult entertainment, or firearms. These restrictions exist to protect minors from products they cannot legally buy and from industries widely seen as inappropriate to associate with school-age athletes.

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation — whether by including a school mark, failing to disclose, or signing a deal that ties compensation to school participation — typically results in loss of amateur status and ineligibility in the affected sport for a period determined by ASAA. Compensation tied to the violating activity may have to be returned, and your school can face its own sanctions if administrators knew about and tolerated the violation.
