# Tennessee — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** TSSAA Amateur Rule (Bylaws Article II, Section 14) — [link](https://tssaa.org/name-image-likeness)

## Overview

Tennessee was one of the earliest states to permit high school NIL. The Tennessee Secondary School Athletic Association (TSSAA) Legislative Council unanimously amended the Amateur Rule in December 2022 to allow student-athletes to earn NIL compensation, effective immediately. The framework is permissive on the athlete side but strict on the school side — schools, coaches, and boosters are firewalled out of the process.

## Who can participate

Any TSSAA student-athlete in good standing. There is no age floor and no sport restriction. You can earn unlimited compensation through endorsements, social-media posts, autograph signings, paid lessons, camp appearances, and instructional services — provided the activity does not suggest your school endorses or sponsors the deal.

If you are a minor, your parent or legal guardian must sign the contract — that is a Tennessee contract-law requirement, not just a TSSAA rule.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm three things:

1. **No school identification.** The deliverable cannot show you in a school uniform or in clothing or gear bearing your school's name, logo, or insignia.
2. **No school facilitation.** Your school, coaches, athletic department, and booster clubs may not solicit, negotiate, or promote the deal. A booster-club payment is treated as suggesting school endorsement and is therefore banned.
3. **No reference to school accolades.** Do not promote yourself in the deal as "[School Name]'s starting quarterback" or reference team championships in the paid content.

Get parental consent in writing before you sign.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Day 0 — sign the contract.** Parent or guardian signs alongside if you're a minor.
2. **Day 0 — strip school identifiers from any creative.** No jersey, no school logo, no facility shots.
3. **Notify your AD.** TSSAA does not impose a specific disclosure window in the bylaw text, but most Tennessee schools expect prompt written notice so they can spot-check compliance. Use NILPro's disclosure template; send it yourself.
4. **Keep your records.** Contract, parental consent, AD notification, every published deliverable.
5. **Re-check after every renewal.** Each new contract is a new compliance event.

## Banned deal categories

- **Anything that puts you in a school uniform or shows school logos, marks, facilities, or trademarks** in the paid content.
- **Booster-club deals.** Payments from anyone reasonably tied to your school carry an endorsement implication and are prohibited.
- **School-arranged deals.** Coaches, ADs, and school staff cannot broker, negotiate, promote, or assist.
- **Collectives connected to a TSSAA member school.**
- **Coach-assisted instruction-for-pay.** Your paid lessons cannot use school facilities or be co-marketed by a school coach.
- **Pay-for-play.** Compensation tied to athletic performance.

Tennessee does not maintain a state-required "vice list" (alcohol, gambling, tobacco, etc.) the way some states do, but those categories also implicate Tennessee minor-contract and advertising laws — avoid them.

## Penalties for non-compliance

- **First violation:** Formal warning; return of awards/money; required removal of violating content.
- **Second violation:** 12-month ineligibility in the sport in which the violation occurred.
- **Third violation:** Additional penalties at the TSSAA Executive Director's discretion based on the nature and intent of the conduct.
