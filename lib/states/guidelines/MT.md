# Montana — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** MHSA Handbook — Section 16 (Amateur Rule, NIL Amendment) — [link](https://www.mhsa.org/resources/handbook/)

## Overview

Montana high school athletes can earn money from name, image, and likeness deals under an amendment to **Section 16 (Amateur Rule)** of the Montana High School Association (MHSA) Handbook, approved by the MHSA membership at the **January 2025 annual meeting**. The amendment took effect for the 2025-2026 school year. Montana's framework is one of the more restrictive in the country: NIL must be entirely disconnected from the school, MHSA, and any team-issued gear, and certain industries — including nicotine/vaping and drug paraphernalia — are explicitly named as prohibited.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete enrolled at an MHSA member school who is otherwise eligible. The amateur rule applies across all MHSA-sanctioned sports and activities.

## Before you sign a deal

- If you are under 18, obtain written parental or guardian consent.
- Confirm no school or MHSA marks, uniforms, gear, or facilities are used.
- Confirm the deal does not use any game film. Montana specifically prohibits the use of game film for NIL purposes — a unique restriction.
- Confirm the brand is not in a banned category (see below).
- Confirm the deal is not pay-for-play or contingent on athletic performance.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. Disclose the agreement to your school's athletic director. Schools may have local notification procedures the AD will walk you through.
2. Do not appear in school or MHSA uniforms, jerseys, warmups, or branded gear in any promotional content — this includes practice gear and travel attire.
3. Do not use any game film, broadcast clips, or in-school photographs in promotional content.
4. Do not promote during MHSA practices, games, tournaments, or team travel.
5. Keep your contracts and payment records — NIL income is taxable in Montana.
6. If the deal is renewed or amended, refresh your disclosure to your school.

## Banned deal categories

- **Nicotine, tobacco, and vaping products** — explicitly named in the MHSA framework. Montana state law also prohibits possession by anyone under 18.
- **Drug paraphernalia** — explicitly named and prohibited.
- **Cannabis products** — prohibited.
- **Alcohol** — prohibited.
- **Sports betting, gambling, and casinos** — prohibited.
- **Adult entertainment** — prohibited because athletes are minors.
- **Firearms, weapons, and ammunition** — prohibited.
- **Performance-enhancing substances** — prohibited.
- **Any deal using school or MHSA logos, marks, uniforms, facilities, or game film** — categorically prohibited even if the brand is otherwise acceptable.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation forfeits your amateur status under Section 16, which makes you ineligible for MHSA-sanctioned interscholastic competition. The MHSA reviews violations through its eligibility process, and reinstatement is not automatic.
