# New York — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** NYSPHSAA Handbook, Rule 2.2(c) (revised October 2021) — [link](https://www.nysphsaa.org/about/handbook)

## Overview

The New York State Public High School Athletic Association (NYSPHSAA) revised its amateur rule in **October 2021** to allow student-athletes to profit from name, image, and likeness. Rule 2.2(c) permits "commercial endorsements" provided there is no school team, school, section, or NYSPHSAA affiliation. New York adds a wrinkle most other states don't: if you are a minor, the **New York Arts and Cultural Affairs Law Article 35 (§§ 35.01–35.03)** governs performance and likeness contracts and may require court approval before the contract is enforceable.

## Who can participate

Any NYSPHSAA-eligible student-athlete may pursue NIL. PSAL (NYC) and Catholic-school student-athletes follow their own associations' rules layered on top of state law — check with your AD if you are not at a NYSPHSAA member school.

## Before you sign a deal

- Strip every reference to your school, team, mascot, uniform, section, league, and NYSPHSAA from the agreement.
- Confirm no school employee, coach, or booster helped arrange the deal.
- **For deals over roughly $1,000, or for any multi-year commitment, talk to a New York attorney before signing.** Article 35 of the Arts and Cultural Affairs Law may require court approval (Surrogate's or Supreme Court) for certain minor contracts; without approval, you may have the right to disaffirm later — but the brand may also walk if it discovers the contract is voidable.
- If a portion of earnings is required to be set aside in trust under §35.03, your lawyer will walk you through guardianship of those savings.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Sign off school property** and outside team activity.
2. **Keep an executed copy** with all exhibits and amendments.
3. **Do not appear in school uniform, gear, or in front of school logos** in any branded content.
4. **Notify your athletic director.** NYSPHSAA does not mandate filing, but member schools and sections commonly require notice; your AD can flag affiliation problems early.
5. **For high-value or multi-year deals,** work with counsel to determine if Article 35 judicial approval is needed. If it is, do not start performance until the court order is in place.
6. **Check ongoing deals each season** to confirm no school-branding drift.

## Banned deal categories

NYSPHSAA's rule centers on the school-affiliation prohibition rather than a federal-style banned-product list, but you may not enter NIL deals that:

- Involve school marks, uniforms, mascots, or facilities
- Reference NYSPHSAA, a section, or a league
- Are arranged by school employees or boosters
- Violate New York law for minors (alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, gambling, firearms, adult content) or your school district's code of conduct

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation may result in **loss of NYSPHSAA eligibility**. Article 35 violations are separate: an unapproved contract may be voidable, and parties who skirt the statute can face civil exposure.
