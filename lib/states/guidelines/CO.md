# Colorado — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** CHSAA Constitution & Bylaws — Article 20, Rules 2000.1-2000.8 (Amateur Status / NIL) — [link](https://chsaanow.com/sports/2021/7/19/bylaws.aspx)

## Overview

The Colorado High School Activities Association (CHSAA) permits high school NIL under its Amateur Status bylaws (Article 20, Rules 2000.1-2000.8), with clarifying language adopted for the 2025-26 school year. The CHSAA's update did not expand or restrict NIL — it tightened the language so athletes, parents, and schools know exactly where the line is. The single most important rule, captured in Bylaw 2000.4: your NIL activity may not have any "connection" to your CHSAA high school, team, or activity program.

## Who can participate

All student-athletes at CHSAA member high schools may participate, across every sanctioned sport and activity. Colorado law (HB25-1041) and CHSAA bylaws together set the framework. There is no minimum age within the bylaw, but Colorado contract law requires a parent or legal guardian to sign for any athlete under 18. The "connection" test applies regardless of grade or sport.

## Before you sign a deal

Apply CHSAA's connection test in advance: would a reasonable viewer of your content connect this deal to your high school, team, or CHSAA activity? Look at every element — the location of the photo or video shoot, the clothing you wear, references in the caption, the brand's marketing copy, even the field where the action takes place. Confirm the deal is not provided by your school, a booster, a school-aligned collective, or anyone acting as an agent for the school or athletic program — those are categorically banned. If you are under 18, plan for a parent or legal guardian to co-sign.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Disclose to your athletic director within 7 days of signing.** CHSAA does not mandate a statewide deadline, but 7 days is the conservative standard. Include the brand, deal terms, dates, and a copy of the contract. Email is acceptable unless your district has its own form.
2. **Have a parent or legal guardian co-sign if you are under 18.** Required by Colorado contract law for minors.
3. **Apply the connection test to every piece of content.** No school jersey, no school logo, no school field or facility, no team name, no mascot, no content implying school endorsement. Generic clothing, public locations, no school references — that's the safe pattern.
4. **Refuse deals from your school, boosters, or any school-aligned collective.** These are banned regardless of content.
5. **Save the signed contract, your disclosure email, and any payment records for at least four years.** CHSAA can request documentation if a question arises.

## Banned deal categories

CHSAA prohibits any compensation that is contingent on athletic performance or achievement (e.g., "$500 if you score 20 points") because that turns NIL into pay-for-play. It bans compensation that incentivizes enrollment, retention, or transfer — protecting competitive balance. It bans deals that use school marks, IP, or facilities — protecting school identity. Athletes should also avoid product categories restricted to minors under Colorado law: alcohol, tobacco/vaping, cannabis, controlled substances, gambling and sports betting, adult entertainment, and firearms.

## Penalties for non-compliance

A first violation can result in immediate ineligibility and possible game forfeitures for the team. A second violation can result in permanent eligibility revocation. Athletes may be required to return any compensation tied to the non-compliant deal. Schools whose administrators knew of the violation can face additional CHSAA sanctions.
