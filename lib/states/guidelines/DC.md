# District of Columbia — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** DCSAA NIL Policy Guide for DC High School Student Athletes — [link](https://www.dcsaasports.com/nil-policy-guide/)

## Overview

The District of Columbia State Athletic Association (DCSAA) made the District the 17th jurisdiction to permit high school NIL, with policy language adopted into the DCSAA Handbook and a stand-alone NIL Policy Guide published for athletes and parents. The DCSAA framework allows commercial and marketing endorsements as long as no school team, school, or DCSAA affiliation (name or logo) is visible in the activity. DC's most up-to-date NIL Policy Guide carries an effective date of March 5, 2026.

## Who can participate

All student-athletes at DCSAA member schools may participate, across every sanctioned sport. The rule applies regardless of grade. There is no minimum age in the policy itself, but DC contract law requires a parent or legal guardian to sign any binding contract on behalf of an athlete under 18. The DCSAA's NIL Policy Guide requires that all participants follow the notification process below, regardless of how small the deal is.

## Before you sign a deal

Read the contract end-to-end for any reference, mention, or implied connection to your school, your team, or the DCSAA — including in photography, brand copy, or your social profile description. If the brand wants you in your school jersey, on your school's field, or describing yourself by your team affiliation, the deal cannot proceed as written. Confirm the deal is not an enrollment or retention incentive (categorically banned). If you are under 18, plan for a parent or legal guardian to co-sign — and remember that representation by an agent or attorney is permitted but cannot include negotiation of future college or pro contracts while you are still a HS student-athlete.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Notify the DCSAA and your school's athletic director of every deal — within 7 days of signing.** The DCSAA explicitly states it must be notified of all commercial or marketing endorsements. Include the brand name, deal terms, dates, and a copy of the contract. Email is acceptable; check whether your school requires a specific form.
2. **Have a parent or legal guardian co-sign the contract if you are under 18.** Required by DC contract law for minors.
3. **Use no school logos, names, uniforms, or DCSAA marks in any deal-related content.** Including social media. Generic clothing and off-campus settings are the safe pattern.
4. **Do not use school apparel or equipment that displays school marks for promotional purposes.** Even neutral-looking gear with a small school logo can trigger a violation.
5. **Save the signed contract, your DCSAA notification, and your school disclosure for at least four years.** The DCSAA may request records.

## Banned deal categories

DCSAA's primary categorical ban is on any deal that uses school or DCSAA affiliation, name, or logo — protecting school identity and preventing implied endorsement by the school. Athletes are also expected to avoid product categories restricted to minors under DC law: alcohol, tobacco, vaping/nicotine, cannabis, controlled substances, gambling and sports betting, adult entertainment, and firearms. These categories are widely banned in HS NIL frameworks across the country and DCSAA's published guidance discourages them.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Per the DCSAA, a student-athlete who violates amateur or NIL rules becomes ineligible to participate at the interscholastic level in the sport in which the violation occurred. Reinstatement is possible after a suspension lasting up to the total number of school days the athlete was non-compliant, provided the athlete returns to compliance with all policy provisions. The DCSAA may also require return of compensation tied to the non-compliant deal. Failure to notify the DCSAA is itself a violation.
