# Missouri — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** RSMo § 173.280 (Missouri NIL Statute) and MSHSAA Eligibility Standards — [link](https://revisor.mo.gov/main/OneSection.aspx?section=173.280)

## Overview

Missouri's HS NIL framework is unique: high school athletes may earn NIL compensation **only after signing an athletic letter of intent (NLI) or other written agreement to enroll at a Missouri postsecondary institution**. Until you have signed with an in-state college, you cannot legally accept NIL money in Missouri without losing your high school eligibility. The rule lives in state statute (RSMo § 173.280), and the Missouri State High School Activities Association (MSHSAA) enforces it through its eligibility standards. This is sometimes called Missouri's "Sign and Earn" model.

## Who can participate

You are eligible to do an NIL deal **only if all of the following are true:**

1. You are a student-athlete at an MSHSAA member school.
2. You have signed a National Letter of Intent or another binding written agreement to enroll at a postsecondary institution **located in Missouri** (e.g., Mizzou, Saint Louis University, Missouri State, Lindenwood, etc.). An out-of-state signing does not unlock NIL under Missouri law.
3. You are otherwise in good academic and conduct standing.

**Limited exception for pre-signing discussions:** Missouri statute lets you discuss potential NIL earnings with a Missouri postsecondary institution while still considering enrollment there. You cannot finalize, sign, or be paid before signing the NLI.

## Before you sign a deal

- Confirm in writing that your NLI / enrollment agreement with the Missouri institution is fully executed.
- Discuss the deal with your high school's athletic director **before** signing. MSHSAA strongly advises this and your school may have local procedures.
- If you are under 18, obtain parental or guardian consent.
- Confirm the brand is not in a banned category (see below) and the deal does not reference your high school, MSHSAA, or any school marks.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. Sign only after the NLI/enrollment agreement is in place. Backdating or "handshake" arrangements before signing are violations.
2. Provide a copy of the NIL contract to your high school AD. Local schools may also require a copy of your NLI for the file.
3. Do not use your high school's name, mascot, logo, uniform, or facilities in any promotional content.
4. Do not promote during MSHSAA practices, games, tournaments, or team travel.
5. Keep payment records for taxes — NIL income is taxable to you.

## Banned deal categories

- **Anything tied to athletic performance** as inducement to enroll or transfer — prohibited by statute.
- **Alcohol, tobacco, vaping, and cannabis** — prohibited under MSHSAA chemical health standards.
- **Sports betting, gambling, and casinos** — prohibited.
- **Adult entertainment** — prohibited.
- **Firearms and weapons** — prohibited.
- **Performance-enhancing substances** — prohibited.
- **Any deal that conflicts with existing Missouri law.**

## Penalties for non-compliance

Accepting NIL compensation before you sign with a Missouri postsecondary institution — or signing with an out-of-state school and trying to monetize while still in HS — costs you your MSHSAA amateur status. That means immediate ineligibility for further high school competition. Schools that allow violators to compete may face forfeitures.
