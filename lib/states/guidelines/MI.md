# Michigan — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** MHSAA Personal Branding Activity (PBA) Policy — [link](https://www.mhsaa.com/students/name-image-likeness)

## Overview

Michigan high school athletes can engage in NIL deals under the Michigan High School Athletic Association's "Personal Branding Activity" (PBA) policy. The MHSAA Representative Council expanded the policy on **January 27, 2026**, opening up significantly broader opportunities than the prior pilot framework. The policy is restrictive by design — Michigan permits individual personal branding but blocks anything that looks like pay-for-play or collective activity.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete at an MHSAA member school in grades 7-12. Eligibility-related events such as transfers, undue influence inquiries, or recruiting violations can disqualify you from PBA participation.

## Before you sign a deal

- Get written parental or guardian consent if you are under 18.
- Confirm the deal is **not** contingent on athletic performance, statistics, awards, recognition, team participation, or competition outcomes — these tie-ins are explicitly banned.
- Confirm the deal does not use school names, logos, mascots, trademarks, or any other identifiers.
- Confirm the deal does not involve school facilities, school uniforms, or school social media accounts.
- Confirm the brand is not in a banned category (see below).
- Confirm there is no group, collective, or pay-for-play structure behind the offer.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Within 7 business days** of any PBA agreement, contract, or opportunity being finalized, you must submit the MHSAA PBA Disclosure Form. This is mandatory.
2. The disclosure goes to **both** the MHSAA and your school. Your school's athletic director must acknowledge it.
3. Do not wear or display any school marks in promotional content. Do not film at school facilities.
4. Do not promote during team practices, games, MHSAA tournaments, or team travel.
5. Keep your contracts, payment records, and 1099s for tax filing. NIL income is taxable.
6. If your deal is amended or extended, file an updated disclosure within the same 7-business-day window.

## Banned deal categories

- **Alcohol, tobacco, vaping products, and cannabis** — prohibited; tied to MHSAA's wellness mission and minor-status concerns.
- **Sports betting, gambling apps, fantasy sports for money, and casinos** — prohibited; threatens competitive integrity.
- **Performance-enhancing or illegal substances** — prohibited.
- **Sexually explicit content / adult entertainment** — prohibited.
- **Firearms, weapons, and ammunition** — prohibited.
- **Anything tied to athletic performance, awards, team membership, or game outcomes** — this is the unique Michigan restriction; even an otherwise fine brand cannot pay you "$X per touchdown."
- **NIL collectives or group-based arrangements** — explicitly prohibited.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Failure to disclose, or a violation of category or content restrictions, can result in loss of MHSAA eligibility for the student-athlete. The MHSAA maintains a separate PBA Penalties document detailing escalation. Schools can also be sanctioned for failing to enforce.
