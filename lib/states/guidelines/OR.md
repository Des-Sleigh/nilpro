# Oregon — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** OSAA Rule 8.4 — Awards / Name, Image, Likeness (NIL) — [link](https://www.osaa.org/governance/handbooks/osaa)

## Overview

The Oregon School Activities Association (OSAA) Delegate Assembly approved Rule 8.4's NIL provisions on **October 10, 2022**, effective immediately. The rule permits Oregon high school student-athletes to enter NIL agreements while preserving their amateur status, on the condition that you keep your school identity and team activity completely out of the deal — and that you **disclose every agreement to your member school**.

## Who can participate

Any OSAA-eligible student-athlete at a member school can pursue NIL. Eligibility is preserved as long as each deal follows the rules below and is properly disclosed.

## Before you sign a deal

- Reread the contract and remove every reference to OSAA, your member school, your school's name, mascot, uniform, logo, or insignia.
- Confirm no coach, school employee, or anyone connected to an OSAA-affiliated high school is on the other side of the deal — Oregon explicitly bars compensation arranged by individuals connected to your school.
- Confirm compensation is **not** a "perform-X-get-paid-Y" arrangement — direct performance correlation is prohibited.
- If you are under 18, have your parent or legal guardian review and co-sign.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Sign off school property** and outside team activity.
2. **Keep a complete signed copy** of the agreement.
3. **Disclose the agreement to your member school** — typically your athletic director and/or principal. This is a required step under Rule 8.4. Send the disclosure from your own email and keep the timestamp.
4. **Do not wear OSAA or school marks, logos, or insignias** — and do not wear any apparel or equipment bearing them — for NIL purposes.
5. **Do not reference OSAA, the school, the team, or the school mascot** in NIL content, captions, or scripts.
6. **Do not promote the deal during team activities** — practices, contests, travel, locker rooms, banquets.
7. **Do not use school facilities** for NIL shoots, appearances, or activations.
8. **Re-disclose any amendment** to your AD.

## Banned deal categories

You may not enter NIL agreements that:

- Use OSAA or member-school marks, logos, insignias, or mascots
- Use school uniforms, apparel, or equipment that bears those marks
- Promote anything during team activities
- Use member-school facilities for the deal
- Are tied to athletic performance ("if you score X, you get Y")
- Come from anyone connected to an OSAA-affiliated high school
- Conflict with your local school district's policies (most districts ban tobacco, alcohol, cannabis, gambling, firearms, controlled substances, and adult content)

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation may result in **loss of OSAA eligibility** and termination of the agreement. OSAA reviews each case on its facts. School employees or affiliated parties involved on the brand side may trigger member-school discipline.
