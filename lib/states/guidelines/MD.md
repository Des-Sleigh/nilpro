# Maryland — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** MPSSAA NIL Guidance — [link](https://www.mpssaa.org/assets/1/6/NIL_Guidance.pdf)

## Overview

Maryland public school athletes can earn money from name, image, and likeness deals under guidance issued by the Maryland Public Secondary Schools Athletic Association (MPSSAA). The policy took effect in late 2022, making Maryland one of the first states to formalize HS NIL rules — and the first to outright ban NIL collectives at the high school level. The MPSSAA's stated goal is to protect the educational mission of interscholastic athletics and shield students from exploitation.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete enrolled at an MPSSAA member school who is otherwise eligible to compete may engage in NIL activities. Maryland's framework treats NIL as something you do as an individual — completely separate from your school identity. Eighth graders and middle-schoolers participating in MPSSAA competition are held to the same rules.

## Before you sign a deal

- If you are under 18, get written parental or guardian consent.
- Confirm the deal makes no reference to your school, your local educational agency (LEA), or the MPSSAA.
- Confirm the company is not a banned category (see below) and is not an NIL collective.
- Make sure no school employee, contractor, or volunteer is acting as your agent or arranging the deal — that's specifically prohibited in Maryland.
- If you're working with a third-party agent or business manager, verify they are not affiliated with your school in any way.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. Sign as yourself, not as a representative of your school or team.
2. Keep a copy of the contract and any related communications. Maryland strongly encourages disclosure to your school's athletic director even though formal disclosure forms are administered locally.
3. Do not wear, display, or reference school uniforms, logos, mascots, nicknames, or facilities in any promotional content.
4. Do not perform NIL activity during team practices, contests, MPSSAA tournaments, or while traveling with your team.
5. Pay your taxes. NIL income is taxable; consult a tax professional or your guardian.

## Banned deal categories

- **NIL collectives** — Maryland is the only state with a flat ban. School employees, contractors, and vendors may not form or run collectives.
- **Video games / esports endorsements** — explicitly prohibited under MPSSAA guidance.
- **Alcohol, tobacco, vaping, cannabis, and other controlled substances** — standard vice-industry exclusions.
- **Gambling, sports betting, and casinos** — incompatible with amateur status.
- **Adult entertainment** — prohibited because athletes are minors.
- **Firearms, weapons, ammunition** — prohibited.
- **Performance-enhancing substances or supplements containing NCAA/WADA-banned ingredients.**

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation can result in loss of amateur status, which makes you ineligible to participate in MPSSAA interscholastic athletics. Reinstatement is not automatic — your school and the MPSSAA review violations case by case.
