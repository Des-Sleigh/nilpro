# Washington — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** WIAA Handbook, Eligibility — Amateur Standing Rules — [link](https://www.wiaa.com/wiaa-handbook/)

## Overview

Washington State permits high school NIL through the Washington Interscholastic Activities Association (WIAA). Notably, WIAA staff have stated this is **not a recent rule change** — it is a clarification of the existing Amateur Standing Rules in the handbook, communicated to member schools in 2022. WIAA does not prohibit student-athletes from engaging in NIL commercial activities in their individual capacity, and doing so does not jeopardize amateur status as long as Amateur Standing Rules are followed. Washington has historically had less public visibility on NIL than other permitting states, so the most common compliance failure here is athletes who don't realize the rules apply to them.

## Who can participate

Any student-athlete enrolled at a WIAA member school in good standing. There is no age floor. If you are a minor, your parent or legal guardian must sign your contract under Washington contract law. The Amateur Standing Rules apply across all sports.

## Before you sign a deal

Confirm the deal does not:

- Use your school's name, uniform, mascot, logo, or marks in any commercial deliverable.
- Use the WIAA name or marks.
- Tie compensation to athletic performance ("pay-for-play").
- Function as a recruiting inducement.
- Involve a category restricted by Washington consumer-protection or minor-contract law (see banned categories).

Get parental consent in writing.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

1. **Day 0 — sign with parental consent.** A parent or guardian signature is required for minors.
2. **Day 0 — verify the creative.** No school uniform, no school logo, no WIAA mention, no school facility shoot.
3. **Notify your AD.** WIAA's published guidance does not specify an exact disclosure window. Best practice is to notify your AD in writing within 7 days of signing so the school can confirm compliance with Amateur Standing Rules. Use NILPro's disclosure template and send it from your own email.
4. **Keep records.** Contract, parental consent, AD notification, every deliverable.
5. **Re-verify for renewals.** Each new agreement is a new compliance event.

## Banned deal categories

- **Anything that puts you in a school uniform or shows school logos, marks, or facilities** in the paid content.
- **WIAA references** in promotional material.
- **Pay-for-play.**
- **Recruiting inducements.**
- **School-arranged or booster-funded deals** that imply school sponsorship.
- **Categories addressed by Washington advertising and minor-protection law:** alcohol, tobacco/vaping, cannabis, gambling, firearms, and adult content. WIAA's handbook routes these through the Amateur Standing Rules and Washington general law; avoid them.

## Penalties for non-compliance

Violation of WIAA Amateur Standing Rules results in loss of amateur status and ineligibility for WIAA competition. Reinstatement is at WIAA's discretion and typically requires returning compensation, removing offending content, and serving an ineligibility period before resuming play. Because Washington's framework lives inside the broader Amateur Standing Rules, penalty determinations follow standard amateur-rule enforcement — your school self-reports, WIAA reviews, and a remedy is set case-by-case.
