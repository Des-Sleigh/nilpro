# North Carolina — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions (heavy compliance burden)
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** NCHSAA Name, Image, and Likeness Policy — [link](https://www.nchsaa.org/name-image-and-likeness/)

## Overview

The North Carolina High School Athletic Association (NCHSAA) Board adopted its NIL policy in 2023 and refined it in subsequent meetings. North Carolina has one of the **most procedurally demanding** HS NIL frameworks in the country: parents/legal guardians must be a **party** to the agreement, the NFHS NIL course is required annually for multiple parties, and a copy of every deal must be sent to **five different officials**. Read this page carefully before you sign anything.

## Who can participate

Any NCHSAA-eligible student-athlete (and, if you are a minor, your parent/legal guardian as a co-party) can engage in NIL — but only after the annual training and disclosure requirements below are satisfied.

## Before you sign a deal

1. **Complete the NFHS Name, Image and Likeness Course annually, before your first contest.** This is required for: the student-athlete, the parent/legal guardian (if the student is under 18), the head coach, the athletic director, and the principal at your school.
2. **Make sure your parent or legal guardian is named as a PARTY to the agreement** — not as a witness, signatory, or notary acknowledgment. They must have rights and obligations under the contract. If you are under 18 and the brand will not add your guardian as a party, walk away.
3. **Confirm the agreement contains a release of liability** for the school district, NCHSAA, the NC Department of Public Instruction, and the NC State Board of Education.
4. **Confirm compensation is not conditioned on athletic performance** or the extent of your participation. Pay-for-play is forbidden.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

NCHSAA disclosure is **multi-party**. Be precise.

1. **Both the student and the parent/legal guardian sign** the agreement as parties.
2. **Before the agreement becomes official,** deliver a complete copy to all five of the following:
   - Your **head coach** (every sport you participate in)
   - Your school's **athletic director**
   - Your school's **principal**
   - The **local superintendent**
   - The **chairperson of the local board of education**
3. **Keep proof of delivery** for each (email timestamps, certified mail receipts). NILPro generates the disclosure email template, but you must send it from your own (or your guardian's) email.
4. **Re-confirm annual NFHS course completion** for student, parent/guardian, head coach, AD, and principal each year before competing.
5. **Do not perform the deal in school uniform, with school marks, on school facilities, or during team activities.**
6. **Resubmit a fresh disclosure for any amendment** to the agreement.

## Banned deal categories

NCHSAA prohibits NIL compensation that:

- Is conditioned on athletic performance, statistics, or playing time (pay-for-play)
- Involves school marks, uniforms, logos, mascots, or facilities
- Comes from a school employee, coach, booster, or anyone acting as an agent of the school
- Conflicts with NCHSAA bylaws or local district conduct policies (alcohol, tobacco, gambling, cannabis, firearms, adult entertainment, controlled substances)

## Penalties for non-compliance

A violation can result in **loss of NCHSAA eligibility**, requirement to terminate the agreement, and potential discipline of school employees who became involved. Failure to complete the annual NFHS course makes you ineligible to enter into any NIL agreement until completed.
