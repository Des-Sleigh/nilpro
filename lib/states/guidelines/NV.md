# Nevada — High School NIL Guidelines

**Status:** Permitted with restrictions
**Last verified:** 2026-04-29
**Primary source:** NIAA Bylaws — Amateur Status & NIL provisions — [link](https://www.niaa.com/landing/index)

## Overview

Nevada has one of the most unusual HS NIL frameworks in the country. The Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association (NIAA) only permits NIL activity for sports it does **not** sanction. If you compete in any of the NIAA's sanctioned sports — football, basketball, baseball, softball, volleyball, soccer, wrestling, track & field, cross country, golf, tennis, swimming & diving, skiing, bowling, and flag football — you cannot monetize your NIL without forfeiting your amateur status. If you compete only in a non-sanctioned sport (for example, certain club or emerging sports outside the NIAA's umbrella), you have full NIL access. The policy was approved in April 2023 and is rooted in NIAA's strict reading of the amateur rule.

## Who can participate

- **Athletes in NIAA-sanctioned sports:** Cannot do NIL deals tied to their athletic skills or reputation while remaining eligible. Period.
- **Athletes who only compete in non-NIAA-sanctioned sports:** May freely engage in NIL activity, subject to general state and federal law.
- **Multi-sport athletes:** If you play *any* NIAA-sanctioned sport, you are bound by the prohibition for the duration of your participation. You cannot "switch off" your eligibility for one sport while monetizing through another.

## Before you sign a deal

If you play an NIAA-sanctioned sport, do not sign. The NIAA does not have a disclosure or waiver pathway that lets sanctioned-sport athletes opt in.

If you only compete in a non-sanctioned sport:

- Get written parental or guardian consent if you are under 18.
- Confirm the deal does not use NIAA marks, school marks, school uniforms, or school facilities.
- Confirm the brand is not in a banned category (see below).
- Verify with your school's AD that your sport is in fact non-sanctioned by NIAA. The list of sanctioned sports changes — check at the start of each school year.

## When you sign a deal — your obligations

(Applies to non-sanctioned-sport athletes only.)

1. Provide a copy of the contract to your school's athletic director.
2. Do not appear in any school uniform, school-issued warmup, or NIAA-branded apparel in promotional content.
3. Do not promote during practices, competitions, or travel for any NIAA-related activity.
4. Do not perform NIL activity in a way that conflicts with school sponsorship or apparel contracts.
5. Track payments for tax purposes.

## Banned deal categories

- **Alcohol, tobacco, vaping, and cannabis** — prohibited.
- **Sports betting, gambling, and casinos** — prohibited; particularly sensitive given Nevada's gaming industry footprint.
- **Adult entertainment** — prohibited because athletes are minors.
- **Firearms, weapons, and ammunition** — prohibited.
- **Performance-enhancing substances** — prohibited.
- **Any deal using NIAA, school, or team marks** — prohibited.
- **For sanctioned-sport athletes: every category** — the prohibition is on the act, not on the brand.

## Penalties for non-compliance

A sanctioned-sport athlete who accepts NIL compensation forfeits amateur status and becomes ineligible for further NIAA competition. A non-sanctioned-sport athlete who violates the content or category rules can also lose amateur status. Reinstatement is governed by NIAA's eligibility process and is not automatic.
